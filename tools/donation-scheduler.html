Donation Schedule FAQ

How often can I donate plasma? +
FDA regulations allow up to 2 plasma donations within a 7-day period, with at least 48 hours between donations. This means you can donate a maximum of 104 times per year. Most donors find a twice-weekly schedule (like Monday and Thursday or Tuesday and Friday) works best.
Why do I need 48 hours between donations? +
The 48-hour rule gives your body time to regenerate plasma proteins. While plasma itself regenerates within 24-48 hours, the FDA requires this waiting period to ensure donor safety. Donating too frequently can lead to fatigue, low protein levels, and other health issues.
What's the best schedule for maximum earnings? +
For maximum earnings, donate twice per week every week. Popular schedules include Monday/Thursday, Tuesday/Friday, or Wednesday/Saturday. The key is consistency - missing donations means losing potential income. Our scheduler helps you visualize the optimal days based on your preferences.
Do new donors earn more than returning donors? +
Yes, new donors typically earn significantly more during their first 4-8 weeks due to new donor bonus programs. After completing the bonus period, you become a returning donor with standard rates. This is why many donors use the bonus stacking strategy to maximize earnings.
What happens if I miss my scheduled donation? +
Most centers don't penalize you for missing appointments, but you lose that potential income and may miss out on bonus promotions. Some centers require you to reschedule within the same week to maintain certain bonus tiers. It's best to cancel appointments in advance if you can't make it.
