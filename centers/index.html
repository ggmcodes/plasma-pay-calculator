Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find plasma centers near me?
Use the search bar above to enter your city or zip code, or click "Use My Location" to find the closest plasma donation centers. We list 2,500+ locations nationwide with real-time pay rates, hours, and bonus information.
How much do plasma centers pay near me?
Plasma donation pay varies by center and location, typically ranging from $50-$100 per visit. New donors can earn $900-$1,200 in their first month through special bonuses. Use our plasma pay calculator to estimate your specific earnings.
Which plasma center pays the most money?
CSL Plasma and BioLife generally offer the highest pay rates at $50-$100 per visit, with new donor bonuses up to $1,200. However, pay varies significantly by location. See our complete pay ranking for a detailed comparison.
What are the requirements to donate plasma?
You must be 18-69 years old, weigh at least 110 lbs, be in good health, and pass a medical screening. You'll need a valid ID, proof of address, and your Social Security number. Most health conditions and medications are acceptable as long as they're stable.
How long does a plasma donation take?
Your first visit typically takes 2-3 hours due to the medical screening and paperwork. Subsequent visits take about 45-90 minutes for the actual donation process. Most donors read, watch shows, or browse their phones during the procedure.
Can I donate plasma at multiple centers?
No ‚Äî donating at multiple plasma companies simultaneously is illegal and tracked by the National Donor Deferral Registry (NDDR). You can switch centers or donate at different locations of the same chain while traveling. Read our complete legal guide.
How often can I donate plasma?
FDA regulations allow plasma donation up to twice per week, with at least 48 hours between donations. This means you can donate up to 8-9 times per month, earning $400-$800+ depending on your center and weight class.
Do plasma centers test for drugs?
Plasma centers screen donated plasma for infectious diseases (HIV, Hepatitis B/C, etc.) but do not typically conduct standard drug panels. However, arriving visibly impaired will result in deferral. Read our complete drug testing guide.
