Common Questions

Is plasma donation really a "side hustle"?
Yes. You're exchanging your time for money - the definition of work. The FDA allows up to 2 donations per week, and centers pay $50-120 per visit. That's $400-960/month for 12-16 hours of passive time where you can read, watch shows, or work on a laptop.
Why don't more people know about this?
Plasma centers don't advertise like Uber or DoorDash. There's also stigma around "selling plasma." But financially, it's one of the highest-paying flexible work options available - especially for the time invested.
Do I have to pay taxes on plasma income?
Yes, plasma payments are taxable income. Most centers issue 1099-NEC forms if you earn over $600/year. The good news: you can deduct mileage to/from the center at $0.70/mile in 2026. See our tax guide for details.
Can I do plasma AND gig work?
Absolutely - and this is the optimal strategy. Max out plasma donations first (2x/week = highest hourly rate), then add gig work for additional income. Just schedule recovery time between plasma sessions.
