💰 Income & Earnings Questions

Is plasma donation income taxable?

Yes, plasma donation income is considered taxable income by the IRS. You will receive a 1099-MISC form if you earn $600 or more per year from a plasma center. We recommend:

  • Setting aside 20-25% of your earnings for taxes
  • Keeping records of all donations and payments
  • Tracking mileage and expenses for potential deductions
  • Consulting a tax professional for specific advice
How accurate is this calculator?

Our calculator provides estimates based on current rates from plasma centers across the United States. However, actual earnings may vary due to:

  • Individual eligibility factors
  • Center-specific policies and promotions
  • Location-based rate differences
  • Weight and health requirements
  • Frequency of donations and bonus cycles

We update our rates weekly, but always verify current rates by calling the plasma center directly.

Why do payments vary by center?

Plasma center payments vary based on several factors:

  • Local Competition: Areas with multiple centers often pay more
  • Demand: Centers in high-demand areas may offer higher rates
  • Operating Costs: Rent, staffing, and other costs affect pricing
  • Corporate Policies: Different companies have different pay structures
  • Donor Supply: Areas with fewer donors may offer higher incentives
What are new donor bonuses?

New donor bonuses are special promotional rates offered to first-time donors, typically for their first 4-8 donations. These can range from $800-1200 total. Benefits include:

  • Higher pay rates for initial visits
  • Completion bonuses for finishing all visits
  • Referral bonuses for bringing friends

Bonus amounts and terms vary by center and change frequently, so call ahead to confirm current offers.

📋 Requirements & Eligibility

How often can I donate plasma?

FDA regulations allow plasma donation up to twice per week with at least 48 hours between donations. This means:

  • Maximum 2 donations per 7-day period
  • Minimum 48 hours between donations
  • Most donors can complete 8-9 donations per month
  • Centers track your donation history electronically
What are the basic requirements for plasma donation?

Basic requirements include:

  • Age: 18-65 years old (some centers up to 69)
  • Weight: Minimum 110 pounds
  • Health: Good general health, pass medical screening
  • Documents: Valid photo ID, proof of address, Social Security verification
  • Lifestyle: No recent tattoos/piercings (12 months), no certain medications
Does weight affect how much I earn?

Yes! Heavier donors can safely give more plasma and typically earn more money:

  • 110-149 lbs: Standard rate (100% of base pay)
  • 150-174 lbs: Medium rate (110% of base pay)
  • 175+ lbs: High rate (120% of base pay)

This can mean an extra $40-80 per month for heavier donors.

🏥 Safety & Health

Is donating plasma safe?

Yes, plasma donation is generally safe when done at FDA-regulated centers. Safety measures include:

  • Sterile, single-use equipment for each donation
  • Comprehensive health screening before each donation
  • Trained medical staff overseeing the process
  • Regular monitoring during donation
  • Strict cleanliness and safety protocols

Your body replaces the donated plasma within 24-48 hours.

What should I do before donating?

To prepare for donation:

  • Hydrate: Drink 16-20 oz of water 2 hours before
  • Eat: Have a protein-rich meal 2-3 hours prior
  • Sleep: Get adequate rest the night before
  • Avoid: Alcohol, fatty foods, and excessive caffeine
  • Bring: ID, proof of address, and entertainment
Are there side effects?

Most people experience minimal side effects. Common, temporary effects may include:

  • Mild fatigue or dizziness
  • Bruising at the needle site
  • Temporary lowered energy levels

Serious side effects are rare. Always inform staff immediately if you feel unwell during or after donation.
