Frequently Asked Questions - Manhattan Plasma Donation

How much can I make donating plasma in Manhattan?

At CSL Plasma Midtown Manhattan, you can earn $75-$125 per donation based on your weight. New donors can earn up to $1,200 in their first month with special bonuses. Regular donors typically earn $600-$800 monthly by donating twice weekly (the maximum allowed).

Is CSL Plasma the only center in Manhattan?

Yes, CSL Plasma Midtown is currently the only plasma donation center located directly in Manhattan. However, there are other centers in nearby boroughs like BioLife in Queens and Octapharma in Brooklyn, all accessible via NYC public transportation.

What are the busiest times at CSL Midtown?

The busiest times are typically weekday evenings (5-7 PM) and weekend afternoons. For shorter wait times, visit on weekday mornings (6-9 AM) or early afternoons (1-3 PM). Book appointments online to guarantee your spot and minimize waiting.

Can tourists donate plasma in NYC?

No, you must be a U.S. resident with a valid Social Security number and proof of local address to donate plasma. International visitors and tourists are not eligible. You'll need a New York State ID or driver's license plus proof of NYC address.

How do I get to CSL Plasma Midtown by subway?

CSL Plasma Midtown is highly accessible by subway. Take the A/C/E lines to 42nd St-Port Authority, the N/Q/R/W to Times Square, or the 1/2/3 to Times Square-42nd St. The center is a 2-3 minute walk from any of these stations.
