Can You Donate Plasma With Sleep Apnea?

Yes, most people with sleep apnea can donate plasma in 2026 if:

  • Treated: Using CPAP or other treatment
  • Compliant: Using treatment consistently
  • No complications: No heart problems from sleep apnea
  • Feel rested: Getting quality sleep

CPAP Users

Good news for CPAP users:

  • Treatment is positive: Shows condition is managed
  • Compliance matters: Regular use demonstrates control
  • Better health: CPAP reduces cardiovascular risk
  • More energy: Good sleep = better donation experience

CPAP Compliance Tips

  • Use CPAP regularly (especially night before donation)
  • Get quality sleep before donating
  • Mention CPAP use during screening
  • Ensure no related heart conditions

Why Untreated Sleep Apnea May Concern Centers

Untreated sleep apnea can cause:

  • High blood pressure: May exceed donation limits
  • Heart problems: Arrhythmias, heart disease
  • Excessive fatigue: May affect donation experience
  • Low oxygen levels: Can affect health

Tips for Donors With Sleep Apnea

  1. Use CPAP: Especially the night before donation
  2. Good sleep: Get quality rest before appointment
  3. Check blood pressure: Know your numbers
  4. Disclose condition: Be honest during screening
  5. Stay hydrated: Extra important if taking BP meds
  6. Morning appointments: When you're most rested

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to bring my CPAP to the donation center?

No, you don't need to bring your CPAP. The center just needs to know you have sleep apnea and how you're treating it.

I was just diagnosed with sleep apnea - can I donate?

You may want to wait until you're established on treatment and know your blood pressure is controlled. Recent diagnosis may require a brief waiting period.

My sleep apnea is mild and I don't use CPAP - can I still donate?

Mild sleep apnea without treatment may be acceptable if you have no related complications like high blood pressure or heart issues. Be honest during screening.

Does plasma donation affect my sleep apnea?

Plasma donation doesn't directly affect sleep apnea. However, if you're dehydrated or fatigued after donation, get extra rest and use your CPAP as usual.

Good News

Treated sleep apnea rarely disqualifies you from plasma donation. Using your CPAP shows you're managing your health responsibly.
