Quick Answer

NO

Can You Donate Plasma With HIV?

No, individuals with HIV cannot donate plasma in 2026. This is an FDA regulation that applies to all plasma centers in the United States. Key points:

  • Permanent deferral: HIV+ status is a lifetime disqualification
  • All centers: This applies to every plasma center
  • Regardless of treatment: Even undetectable viral load
  • Safety requirement: Protects plasma recipients

Why This Rule Exists

The restriction exists for safety reasons:

  • Plasma is used for medications: People with compromised immune systems receive plasma-derived products
  • Testing window period: HIV may not be detectable immediately after infection
  • Recipient safety: Protecting vulnerable patients
  • FDA regulation: Federal requirement for all centers

HIV Testing During Donation

All plasma donations are tested:

  • Initial screening: Questions about HIV status and risk factors
  • Blood testing: Every donation is tested for HIV
  • Notification: You will be notified if tests are positive
  • Confidential: Results are kept private

If You Test Positive

If HIV is detected during screening:

  • You will be notified privately
  • Your plasma will not be used
  • You will be permanently deferred
  • You should seek medical care

Alternative Ways to Help

If you have HIV and want to contribute to health causes:

  • HIV research studies: Many studies compensate participants
  • Clinical trials: For HIV treatments
  • Advocacy: Support HIV/AIDS organizations
  • Volunteer: Help at local health organizations
  • Fundraising: Support plasma/blood research

Resources

Helpful organizations and information:

  • HIV.gov: Official government resource
  • CDC: HIV testing and prevention information
  • Local health department: Testing and resources
  • AIDS Healthcare Foundation: Care and support

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my viral load is undetectable?

Unfortunately, even with undetectable viral load and successful treatment, plasma donation is not permitted. The FDA regulation applies regardless of treatment status.

Can I donate blood instead?

No, the same restriction applies to blood donation. HIV+ individuals cannot donate any blood products.

Will I be discriminated against?

Centers handle deferrals confidentially and professionally. Your HIV status is protected health information.

Important

If you're unsure of your HIV status, please get tested at a local health clinic before attempting to donate plasma. Free and confidential testing is widely available.
