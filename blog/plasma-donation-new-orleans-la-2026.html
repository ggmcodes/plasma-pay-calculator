New Orleans Donor Tips

  1. Tulane students: Green Wave - private school is expensive
  2. UNO students: Privateers - state school savings
  3. Hospitality workers: Restaurant/hotel industry
  4. Mardi Gras: February is VERY busy - plan ahead
  5. Jazz Fest: April/May brings crowds

University Corridor

Uptown has multiple schools:

  • Tulane: 14,000+ students, prestigious
  • Loyola: 5,000+ students, Jesuit tradition
  • Xavier: HBCU, pharmacy program
  • SUNO: Southern University at New Orleans

Tourism Economy

Service industry dominates:

  • Restaurants: Famous culinary scene
  • Hotels: Tourism employs thousands
  • Music venues: Entertainment industry
  • Variable hours: Plasma fits service schedules

Festival Season

Plan around major events:

  • Mardi Gras: February - city is chaos
  • Jazz Fest: Late April/early May
  • French Quarter Fest: April
  • Essence Fest: July 4th weekend

New Orleans Unique Challenges

NOLA considerations:

  • Humidity: Stay extra hydrated
  • Heat: Summer is brutal
  • Late nights: City sleeps late, centers may too
  • Alcohol culture: Skip 24-48 hrs before donating

NOLA vs. Baton Rouge

Louisiana options:

  • New Orleans: Tourism, private universities
  • Baton Rouge: LSU Tigers, state capital
