🎯 Iowa Plasma Donation Requirements

Basic Requirements

  • Age: 18-69 years old (weight dependent)
  • Weight: Minimum 110 pounds (higher weight = more pay)
  • Health: Pass medical screening and physical exam
  • ID: Valid photo ID and proof of address
  • Frequency: Maximum twice per week, 48 hours apart
  • Travel History: No recent travel to certain countries

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much do first-time donors get in Iowa?

First-time donors in Iowa can earn $800-$1,200 in their first month through new donor bonus programs. Most centers offer elevated rates for the first 5-8 donations.

Which Iowa plasma center pays the most?

BioLife Plasma typically offers the highest rates in Iowa, with regular payments of $60-$80 per visit plus substantial new donor bonuses.

Can you donate plasma twice in one week in Iowa?

Yes, Iowa follows federal guidelines allowing plasma donation twice per week with at least 48 hours between donations.

How long does plasma donation take in Iowa?

First visit: 2-3 hours (includes screening)
Regular visits: 60-90 minutes

🛡️ Donor Rights and Legal Protections

✅ Your Rights as a Donor

  • • Right to Information: Full disclosure of risks, procedures, and compensation
  • • Right to Refuse: Stop donation at any time without penalty
  • • Right to Privacy: Medical information protected under HIPAA
  • • Right to Compensation: Receive agreed payment for completed donations
  • • Right to Safety: Sterile equipment and trained medical staff
  • • Right to Comfort: Breaks, restroom access, and refreshments

⚖️ Legal Protections
