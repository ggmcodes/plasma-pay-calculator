Can You Donate Plasma With Migraines?

Yes, migraines generally don't disqualify you from plasma donation in 2026:

  • History of migraines: Not disqualifying
  • Active migraine: Wait until it resolves
  • Most medications OK: Triptans, preventives acceptable
  • Between attacks: You can donate normally

Why Migraines Don't Disqualify

  • Neurological condition: Doesn't affect blood/plasma
  • Not transmissible: Can't spread through plasma
  • Common condition: 39 million Americans have migraines
  • Intermittent: Between attacks, you're healthy

Avoiding Donation-Triggered Migraines

Plasma donation could trigger migraines if you're not careful:

  1. Stay hydrated: Dehydration is a major trigger
  2. Eat well: Don't skip meals before donation
  3. Sleep enough: Fatigue triggers migraines
  4. Avoid fasting: Blood sugar drops can trigger
  5. Bright lights: Centers may have fluorescent lighting

Hydration is Critical

For migraine sufferers donating plasma:

  • Day before: Extra water intake
  • Morning of: At least 16oz water
  • After donation: Replenish fluids immediately
  • Electrolytes: Sports drinks can help

Center Environment

Things that might trigger migraines at donation centers:

  • Fluorescent lights: Bring sunglasses if needed
  • Noise: TVs, conversations - bring headphones
  • Temperature: Centers can be cold
  • Smells: Antiseptic, other donors' perfume

Frequently Asked Questions

I get chronic migraines (15+ days/month) - can I still donate?

Yes, if you're between migraines and feeling well. Chronic migraine itself isn't disqualifying. Just don't donate during an active attack.

I take Aimovig monthly - is that a problem?

No, CGRP inhibitors like Aimovig, Ajovy, and Emgality are generally acceptable for plasma donation. These are preventive medications that don't affect plasma quality.

Will donating plasma trigger a migraine?

It can if you don't prepare properly. Dehydration and low blood sugar are common migraine triggers. Drink plenty of water, eat well, and rest afterward to minimize risk.

Can I bring sunglasses to wear during donation?

Yes! If fluorescent lights bother you, wearing sunglasses during donation is perfectly acceptable. Staff will understand.

Migraine-Friendly Tips

Migraines don't prevent plasma donation, but take extra care to hydrate and eat well. Consider bringing sunglasses and headphones to make the experience more comfortable.
