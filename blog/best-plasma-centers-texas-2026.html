Houston: Most Plasma Centers in Texas

Houston leads the state with 40+ plasma donation centers, offering donors maximum flexibility and competition-driven pay rates. The city's sprawling geography means you're rarely more than 15 minutes from a donation center.

CSL Plasma operates 20+ locations throughout Greater Houston, making it the dominant player. With centers from The Woodlands to Pearland, CSL offers consistent pay structures and minimal wait times due to their extensive network.

Texas-Specific Plasma Donation Regulations

No Tattoo Waiting Period

Texas is one of the most donor-friendly states regarding tattoo eligibility. Unlike states requiring 3-12 month waiting periods after tattoos, Texas allows immediate donation if the tattoo was done at a licensed facility.

  • Licensed facility tattoos: No waiting period
  • Unlicensed or out-of-state: May require 3-12 month wait
  • Why this matters: More eligible donors, higher earning potential

Texas Health and Safety Standards

Texas Department of State Health Services oversees plasma centers with strict cleanliness and safety protocols.

  • Regular inspections of all centers
  • Mandatory staff training requirements
  • Equipment sterilization protocols
  • Donor screening procedures

Donor Frequency Limits

Texas follows federal FDA guidelines allowing twice-weekly donations with minimum 48-hour intervals between donations.

  • Maximum frequency: 2 donations per week, 8 per month, 104 per year
  • Minimum interval: 48 hours between donations
  • Enforcement: Tracked by national donor database preventing center-hopping

Age Requirements in Texas

  • Minimum age: 18 years old (16-17 with parental consent at some centers)
  • Maximum age: No upper limit if health permits
  • ID requirements: Government-issued photo ID, proof of address, Social Security card or proof of SSN

Frequently Asked Questions

Which plasma center pays the most in Texas?

BioLife and Octapharma typically offer the highest new donor bonuses in Texas, with promotions reaching $1,000-1,200 in first month. CSL Plasma and Grifols/Biomat have larger footprints with consistent $50-120 per donation for returning donors. Austin BioLife locations offer the highest regular rates due to local competition.

How many plasma donation centers are in Texas?

Texas has over 200 plasma donation centers statewide as of 2026. Houston has 40+ locations, Dallas/Fort Worth has 35+, San Antonio has 15+, Austin has 12+, and El Paso has 8+ centers. Smaller cities like Lubbock, Corpus Christi, and Amarillo have 3-5 centers each.

Does Texas have a waiting period after getting a tattoo to donate plasma?

No. Texas does not require a waiting period after getting a tattoo from a licensed facility. This makes Texas one of the most donor-friendly states for plasma donation eligibility. Tattoos from unlicensed facilities or out-of-state may still require a 3-12 month waiting period depending on the center's policy.

What is the best plasma center in Houston?

CSL Plasma dominates Houston with 20+ locations offering consistent pay and minimal wait times. Grifols/Biomat has strong presence in northwest Houston with excellent loyalty programs. BioLife offers competitive new donor bonuses near Galleria and Medical Center. Best choice depends on your location and donor status (new vs. returning).

Can you donate plasma in Texas twice a week?

Yes. Texas follows federal FDA guidelines allowing plasma donation twice per week with at least 48 hours between donations. Most centers operate on a twice-weekly schedule for maximum earnings. Eight donations per month is standard, with many centers offering frequency bonuses for completing all eight.

What plasma centers are in Austin Texas?

Austin has 12+ plasma centers including BioLife (most popular with UT students), CSL Plasma locations on Lamar and Research Boulevard, Grifols/Biomat on Rundberg, and Octapharma in North Austin. BioLife offers highest first-month bonuses ($1,000-1,150) while CSL provides most consistent long-term pay.

How much can you make donating plasma in Texas?

New donors can earn $800-1,200 in first month with promotional bonuses. Regular donors typically earn $200-400 per month donating twice weekly, with heavy donors (175+ lbs) earning $300-500 monthly. Pay varies by weight, center, and location within Texas. Austin pays 10-15% above state average.

Are there plasma centers in El Paso Texas?

Yes. El Paso has 8+ plasma centers including CSL Plasma, Grifols/Biomat, BioLife, and local centers. Border town dynamics create consistent demand with competitive pay rates matching or exceeding state averages. Most centers offer bilingual (English/Spanish) services and staff.
