FDA Donation Rules

The FDA regulates plasma donation frequency for your safety. Here are the official rules:

The Core Rules

  • Maximum: 2 donations per 7-day period
  • Minimum gap: 48 hours between donations
  • Calendar week: Most centers track Sunday-Saturday
  • No exceptions: Centers must follow FDA guidelines

Why These Limits?

  • Protein regeneration: Your body needs time to replace plasma proteins
  • Fluid recovery: Allows proper hydration restoration
  • Safety first: Prevents over-donation complications
  • Quality plasma: Ensures donated plasma is high quality

Health Considerations

Listen to Your Body

  • Feeling tired: Take a week off
  • Dehydration signs: Skip until you're properly hydrated
  • Illness: Wait until fully recovered
  • Stressed: Your body needs resources - maybe skip

Supporting Twice-Weekly Donations

  • Hydration: Drink 8-10 glasses of water daily
  • Protein: Eat adequate protein (50-60g daily)
  • Iron: Include iron-rich foods
  • Sleep: Get 7-8 hours nightly
  • Exercise: Light activity helps circulation

Signs You're Donating Too Often

  • Constant fatigue
  • Bruising that won't heal
  • Dizziness between donations
  • Trouble meeting protein levels at screening
  • Getting deferred frequently

Frequency FAQ

Can I donate 3 times per week?

No. FDA regulations strictly limit plasma donation to twice per 7-day period. No center can allow more.

What if I wait exactly 48 hours?

You can donate. 48 hours is the minimum. The center's system will allow you to donate once 48 hours have passed.

Does donating twice weekly hurt my health?

Not for most people. If you're healthy, well-hydrated, and eating properly, twice-weekly donation is safe. Listen to your body.

Can I donate at two different centers?

No. Centers share a national database. You can only donate at one center, and total donations cannot exceed twice weekly across all centers.

What happens if I try to donate too soon?

The system will stop you. When you check in, the computer will show you're not eligible yet and tell you when you can return.
