Quick Answer

Minimum age for plasma donation is 18 years old at all US centers - no exceptions. Most centers have no strict maximum age limit; as long as you pass health screening, you can donate. Valid photo ID proving age is required.

Minimum Age Requirements

The 18-Year-Old Rule

  • Universal requirement: All plasma centers require donors be 18+
  • No exceptions: Parental consent doesn't change this
  • Federal regulation: Tied to legal consent and payment
  • Applies everywhere: Every state, every center

Why 18 Is the Minimum

  • Legal ability to sign contracts and consent forms
  • Physical maturity for safe plasma extraction
  • Ability to receive compensation legally
  • Federal guidelines for plasma collection

What Matters More Than Age

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum age to donate plasma?

The minimum age is 18 years old at all plasma centers in the United States. Some states allow 16-17 year olds with parental consent for blood donation, but this generally doesn't apply to paid plasma donation.

Is there a maximum age for plasma donation?

Most centers have no strict upper age limit. As long as you pass the health screening and meet eligibility requirements, you can donate at any age. Some centers may have policies around 65-69.

Can 17-year-olds donate plasma for money?

No, paid plasma donation requires being 18 years old. Even with parental consent, minors cannot participate in compensated plasma programs. This is a federal regulation.

Can seniors donate plasma safely?

Yes, many seniors donate plasma regularly. As long as you pass health screening with acceptable vital signs, hematocrit, and protein levels, age alone doesn't disqualify you.

Do I need to show ID proving my age?

Yes, valid government-issued photo ID is required at every plasma center. This verifies your identity and confirms you meet the minimum age requirement of 18.

Why is the minimum age 18 for plasma donation?

The 18+ requirement ensures donors can legally consent to the procedure and payment. It also ensures physical maturity and allows for safe plasma volume extraction based on adult body weight.
