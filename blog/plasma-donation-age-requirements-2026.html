Minimum Age Requirements

The standard minimum age for plasma donation is 18 years old at most centers. However, some states and centers have variations:

Standard Rule: 18 Years Old

  • Federal requirement: Must be at least 18
  • No parental consent: Can't donate under 18 with parent permission
  • Legal adult: Must be able to sign consent forms
  • ID verification: Valid government ID required

Why 18?

  • Legal consent: Must be able to legally sign medical consent
  • Physical maturity: Body should be fully developed
  • Medical history: Need complete adult medical screening

Maximum Age Limits

Good news for older adults: there's often no strict maximum age for plasma donation.

General Guidelines

  • No upper limit: Many centers accept healthy donors of any age
  • Health-based: Eligibility depends on overall health, not just age
  • First-time limits: Some centers have age limits for NEW donors only
  • 66+ restrictions: Some centers require additional health screening for donors over 66

Age-Related FAQ

Can I donate plasma at 17 with parental consent?

No. Unlike blood donation at some locations, paid plasma donation requires you to be 18. Parental consent doesn't change this.

Is there an upper age limit for plasma donation?

Generally no. Most centers accept healthy donors of any age. Your eligibility depends on your health status, not your birthday.

Do seniors get paid less for plasma?

No. Pay rates are the same regardless of age. Seniors earn the same as younger donors.

What ID do I need to prove my age?

  • Valid driver's license
  • State ID card
  • Passport
  • Military ID

Can college freshmen donate if they're only 17?

No. Must wait until 18th birthday, even if enrolled in college.
