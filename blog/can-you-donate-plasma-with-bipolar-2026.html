Can You Donate Plasma With Bipolar Disorder?

Yes, bipolar disorder typically doesn't disqualify you from plasma donation in 2026:

  • Mental health: Doesn't affect blood/plasma safety
  • Medications OK: Most bipolar meds acceptable
  • Not contagious: Can't transmit through plasma
  • Stability matters: Donate when stable

Why Bipolar Doesn't Disqualify

  • Brain condition: Affects mood, not blood quality
  • Not infectious: Mental health can't spread
  • Common: 2.8% of US adults have bipolar
  • Managed: Most people function well with treatment

Importance of Stability

Donate when stable:

  • Euthymic state: Neither manic nor depressed
  • Regular meds: Taking medication consistently
  • Sleep: Getting adequate rest
  • Decision-making: Clear-headed judgment

When NOT to Donate

Skip donation during:

  • Manic episodes: Impaired judgment, impulsivity
  • Depressive episodes: Low energy, may feel worse after
  • Med changes: Wait until stable on new regimen
  • Hospitalization: Focus on recovery first

Donation Tips for Bipolar

  1. Take your meds: Stay on your medication schedule
  2. Donate when stable: Choose good mental health days
  3. Track moods: Know your patterns
  4. Sleep well: Night before donation
  5. Hydrate: Especially if lithium - affects fluid balance
  6. Routine: Regular donation can become positive routine

Lithium and Hydration

Special consideration:

  • Lithium: Sensitive to fluid/sodium changes
  • Hydrate extra: Before and after donation
  • Eat salty foods: Helps with fluid retention
  • Monitor: Watch for signs of lithium toxicity

Frequently Asked Questions

I take lithium - is there special consideration?

Lithium is acceptable for plasma donation, but be extra careful about hydration. Lithium levels are sensitive to fluid and sodium changes. Drink plenty of water before and after donation, and eat salty snacks to help maintain balance.

Will donating affect my mood?

For most people with bipolar disorder, plasma donation doesn't directly affect mood. However, poor sleep, dehydration, or stress can trigger episodes. Donate when you're stable and well-rested.

I was hospitalized last month - can I donate now?

If you were recently hospitalized for a mental health episode, wait until you're fully stable and back to your normal routine. There's no specific waiting period, but prioritize your mental health recovery first.

Do I have to disclose my bipolar diagnosis?

You should disclose it when asked about medical conditions, but bipolar disorder itself won't disqualify you. Be sure to list all medications during screening.

Can plasma donation become a positive routine?

Many people with bipolar benefit from regular routines. Twice-weekly plasma donation at the same times can become a structured, positive habit that also provides income.

Stability First

Bipolar disorder doesn't prevent plasma donation. The key is donating during stable periods, taking your medications consistently, and staying well-hydrated (especially on lithium). Your mental health comes first!
