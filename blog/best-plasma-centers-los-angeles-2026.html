Plasma Donation Requirements in LA

All plasma centers in Los Angeles follow FDA guidelines, but some have additional requirements:

Basic Requirements (All Centers)

  • ✓ Age 18-69 (some centers accept 16-17 with parental consent)
  • ✓ Weight at least 110 lbs
  • ✓ Valid photo ID (California ID, driver's license, or passport)
  • ✓ Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement, or mail dated within 30-60 days)
  • ✓ Social Security number or proof of legal residency
  • ✓ Pass health screening (blood pressure, pulse, temperature, hematocrit)

LA-Specific Note: If you've recently moved, bring multiple forms of address proof. Some centers are strict about address verification in the LA metro area.

LA Donor Tips: Maximize Earnings & Minimize Hassle

  • 1
    Beat the Traffic

    LA traffic can turn a 15-minute drive into an hour. Schedule appointments for early morning (6-8 AM), mid-day (11 AM-2 PM), or late evening to avoid rush hour. Many centers open early and close late to accommodate work schedules.

  • 2
    Compare Nearby Centers

    Pay rates vary significantly between LA locations, even within the same company. A 20-minute drive could mean $10+ more per visit. Use the center apps to compare current promotions before choosing.

  • 3
    Parking Strategy

    Most LA plasma centers have free parking, but lots fill up during peak hours. Arrive 15 minutes early to secure a spot. Some locations near Metro stations are accessible by public transit.

  • 4
    Use the Apps

    BioLife and CSL both have apps with real-time wait times and flash bonuses. Check before heading out to see if there are any surprise promotions. Set notifications for bonus alerts.

  • 5
    Stay Hydrated in LA Heat

    Southern California's dry climate and heat can dehydrate you faster than you realize. Drink extra water the day before and day of donation. This speeds up the donation process and reduces side effects.

  • 6
    Combine with Errands

    Choose a center near grocery stores, gyms, or other regular stops to make the trip more efficient. Many LA plasma centers are in shopping plazas.

  • 7
    New Donor Bonus Strategy

    If you're new to plasma donation, start with the center offering the highest new donor bonus (currently BioLife at $900). After completing your first 8 donations, you can switch to a center with better regular pay or location.

Your First Visit to an LA Plasma Center

Here's what to expect on your first visit:

1

Registration (20-30 min)

Fill out paperwork, provide ID and proof of address, take a photo. Some centers do this online beforehand.

2

Physical Exam (15-20 min)

A staff member will check your vitals, review your health history, and give you a brief physical. This is only required on your first visit.

3

Health Screening (10-15 min)

Answer health questions on a touchscreen or with staff. Get your blood pressure, pulse, and hematocrit checked.

4

Donation (45-90 min)

Sit in a comfortable chair while the machine collects your plasma. Bring headphones, a book, or your phone. First donations may take longer.

5

Payment (Immediate)

Your payment is loaded onto a prepaid debit card. You can use it at ATMs or anywhere debit cards are accepted.

Total first visit time: Plan for 2.5-3 hours. Subsequent visits take 1-1.5 hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which LA plasma center pays the most?

As of January 2026, BioLife offers the highest new donor bonus at $900+ for your first 8 donations. For regular donors, Grifols often has the best per-visit rates, though this varies by location. Always compare current promotions on center apps.

How often can I donate plasma in California?

You can donate plasma up to twice per week (with at least one day between donations) in California. Most donors do 6-8 donations per month to maximize earnings while maintaining good health.

Is plasma donation income taxable in California?

Yes, plasma donation payments are considered taxable income by both the IRS and California's Franchise Tax Board. Centers will issue a 1099 if you earn over $600. Keep track of your earnings for tax purposes.

Can I donate plasma if I'm a UCLA or USC student?

Yes, college students make excellent donors as long as they meet the age and weight requirements. You'll need proof of your current address (dorm mail or utility bill works) and valid ID. Many students donate plasma as a steady income source.

What if I moved recently in LA?

You'll need to update your address with the plasma center. Bring your new proof of address (dated within 30-60 days). Some centers require you to complete this before donating. If you're switching between LA-area locations of the same company, the process is usually quick.

Are there 24-hour plasma centers in LA?

Most LA plasma centers operate from early morning (6-7 AM) until late evening (7-9 PM), but not 24 hours. Some locations have extended hours on weekends. Check with your specific center for their current schedule.

Can I switch between plasma centers in LA?

You can donate at different companies (e.g., BioLife one month, CSL the next), but you can only be registered with one center per company at a time. You cannot donate at two different companies in the same week.
