Grifols vs Biomat USA: What's the Difference?

Many donors are confused about the relationship between Grifols and Biomat USA. Here's the explanation:

Same Company, Different Names

Biomat USA is owned by Grifols. They're the same company operating under different brand names. Some older centers still use the Biomat USA name, while newer centers use Grifols branding. Pay rates, policies, and procedures are identical.

Brand History

  • Grifols - Parent company (Spanish)
  • Biomat USA - Older US brand name
  • Talecris - Another brand Grifols acquired

Grifols Donor Tips

Maximize Your Grifols Experience

  1. Call ahead - Check wait times before going
  2. Go during off-peak hours - Avoid first thing Monday and Friday afternoons
  3. Complete pre-screening - Use the app to save time
  4. Ask about promotions - Staff may not always announce them
  5. Bring entertainment - Wait times can be longer

Comparison Strategy

Smart donors often use this approach:

  1. Start at Octapharma - Get the $1,500 new donor bonus
  2. Try BioLife next - $1,200 bonus, possibly better ongoing rates
  3. Use Grifols - If neither is convenient, or for their $1,000 bonus
Pro Tip

If you've donated at other centers and exhausted their new donor bonuses, Grifols can be a good option for a fresh start. Just verify you meet their "new donor" criteria (usually 6+ months since last donation there).

Grifols FAQ

Are Grifols and Biomat USA the same?

Yes! Biomat USA is owned by Grifols. They're the same company with different brand names. Pay rates and policies are identical.

Why does Grifols pay less than other centers?

Pay rates vary by market and competition. In areas with fewer options, Grifols may be comparable. In competitive markets, they sometimes lag behind BioLife or Octapharma.

Can I walk in without an appointment?

Many Grifols locations accept walk-ins, but wait times can be long. It's usually better to schedule through the app if possible.
