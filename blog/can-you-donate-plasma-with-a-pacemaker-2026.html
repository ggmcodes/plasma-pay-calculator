Can You Donate Plasma With a Pacemaker?

No, individuals with pacemakers cannot donate plasma in 2026. Key points:

  • Permanent deferral: Pacemakers disqualify
  • All centers: Universal policy
  • Safety concern: Underlying heart condition
  • ICDs too: Defibrillators also defer

Why Pacemakers Disqualify

The restriction exists for your safety:

  • Underlying condition: The heart problem requiring the pacemaker is the concern
  • Donation stress: Plasma donation can stress the cardiovascular system
  • Fluid shifts: Changes in blood volume may affect heart rhythm
  • Emergency risk: Higher risk of cardiac events

Heart Conditions That Typically Defer

Beyond devices, heart conditions themselves often disqualify:

  • Heart failure: Permanent deferral
  • Arrhythmias: Depends on type and severity
  • Heart attack history: Often permanent
  • Cardiomyopathy: Usually deferred
  • Congenital heart defects: Case by case

What About Minor Heart Issues?

Some conditions may not defer:

  • Heart murmur (benign): May be OK
  • Mitral valve prolapse (mild): May be OK
  • Controlled AFib: Usually not OK
  • High blood pressure (controlled): Usually OK

Alternative Ways to Help

If you can't donate plasma:

  • Heart research studies: Many compensate participants
  • Clinical trials: For cardiac conditions
  • Advocacy: Support heart health organizations
  • Fundraising: Help cardiac research
  • Volunteer: Non-physical healthcare support

Frequently Asked Questions

My pacemaker is for a minor condition - can I still donate?

No. Having a pacemaker is a permanent deferral regardless of the underlying condition's severity. The device itself indicates a cardiac condition requiring treatment.

What if I had a pacemaker removed?

This would require individual evaluation. The underlying condition that required the pacemaker would still be a concern. Contact centers directly for guidance.

Can plasma donation affect my pacemaker?

The plasmapheresis equipment shouldn't directly affect pacemaker function, but the fluid shifts and cardiovascular stress of donation make it unsafe for pacemaker patients.

Important

Always prioritize your heart health. Never attempt to hide a pacemaker or heart condition during screening.
