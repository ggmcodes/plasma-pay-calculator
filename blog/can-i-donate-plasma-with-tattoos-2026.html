Tattoo Rules in 2026

The Key: State Regulation

Whether you can donate immediately after a tattoo depends on where you got it:

Regulated vs. Unregulated States

If your tattoo was done at a state-licensed facility using sterile equipment, most plasma centers will accept you immediately. If the state doesn't regulate tattoo shops, you may need to wait 3-4 months.

States with Tattoo Regulations (No Wait Required)

Most states now regulate tattoo parlors. As of 2026, the following typically require NO waiting period for tattoos from licensed shops:

  • California, Texas, Florida, New York
  • Illinois, Pennsylvania, Ohio, Georgia
  • North Carolina, Michigan, Arizona
  • Most other states with health department oversight

State-by-State Tattoo Regulations

Check Your State

Regulations change. When in doubt, bring documentation from your tattoo shop (receipt, license number) to verify it was a regulated facility.

Some states have less comprehensive tattoo regulation. If you're unsure, ask the plasma center about your specific state.

Tips for Tattooed & Pierced Donors

  1. Keep your receipt - Shows date, shop name, and helps verify licensing
  2. Know your state's rules - Ask if your state regulates tattoo shops
  3. Be honest - Disclose all tattoos and piercings during screening
  4. Wait if unsure - If you got tattooed abroad or informally, wait the full 3-4 months
  5. Check for healing - New tattoos should be fully healed (no scabbing)

Frequently Asked Questions

I have lots of tattoos - is that a problem?

No. The number of tattoos doesn't matter. Only the recency and source of your most recent tattoo matters for eligibility.

What if I got a touch-up on an old tattoo?

A touch-up counts as a new tattoo. The same waiting period rules apply based on where it was done.

Can I donate if I have permanent makeup?

Permanent makeup (eyebrows, eyeliner, lip color) is considered a tattoo. Same rules apply - no wait if done at a licensed facility in a regulated state.

What about microblading?

Microblading is treated like a tattoo. If done at a licensed facility, usually no waiting period. If uncertain, wait 3-4 months.

I got a tattoo in another country - what now?

Foreign tattoos typically require a 3-4 month waiting period regardless of the shop's reputation, as centers can't verify foreign licensing.

What if my tattoo got infected?

Wait until the infection is completely resolved and the tattoo is fully healed. You may need additional deferral time beyond the standard wait.
