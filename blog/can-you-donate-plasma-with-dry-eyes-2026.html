Frequently Asked Questions

I have chronic dry eye syndrome - can I donate plasma?

Yes! Dry eye syndrome doesn't prevent plasma donation. It's an eye condition that has no impact on your blood or plasma quality and isn't contagious. Continue using your eye drops as usual.

I use Restasis for dry eyes - is that OK?

Yes, prescription eye drops like Restasis (cyclosporine), Xiidra (lifitegrast), and Cequa are acceptable for plasma donation. They work locally in your eyes and don't affect your plasma.

Will donation make my dry eyes worse?

Mild dehydration from donation could temporarily worsen symptoms. Stay well-hydrated before and after donating. Bring your eye drops and use them as needed. The air conditioning in centers can also be drying.

Can I use eye drops during donation?

Yes, you can use artificial tears or your prescription eye drops during donation if needed. Your free hand will be available for this. Bring your drops with you if you're prone to dry eye discomfort.
