GCAM Plasma Donor Requirements

Before visiting a GCAM Plasma center, ensure you meet all eligibility requirements. Missing any of these can result in deferral and wasted time.

Basic Eligibility Requirements

  • Age: 18-69 years old (some locations 18-64)
  • Weight: Minimum 110 lbs
  • Health: Generally good health, free from infectious diseases
  • Medications: Most medications are acceptable; some may cause deferral
  • Recent tattoos/piercings: May need to wait 3-12 months depending on state
  • Travel history: Recent travel to certain countries may cause temporary deferral

Required Documentation

Bring these documents to your first GCAM Plasma appointment:

  1. Photo ID: Valid, unexpired government-issued ID (driver's license, state ID, passport)
  2. Proof of Social Security Number: Social Security card or official document with SSN
  3. Proof of Current Address: Utility bill, bank statement, or mail postmarked within last 30 days

All documents must be current and valid. Expired IDs will not be accepted. The name on your Social Security card must match your photo ID exactly.

Medical Screening Process

GCAM Plasma conducts comprehensive medical screening to ensure donor safety:

  • Blood pressure: Must be within acceptable range (typically 90/50 to 180/100)
  • Pulse rate: 50-100 beats per minute
  • Temperature: Below 99.5Â°F
  • Protein levels: Finger-stick test; must meet minimum threshold (usually 6.0 g/dL)
  • Hematocrit: Measures red blood cell percentage; must be 38% or higher
  • Physical exam: First visit only; checks for vein quality, track marks, overall health
  • Medical history: Review of health conditions, medications, travel, and lifestyle factors

Disqualifying Conditions

Certain conditions permanently or temporarily prevent plasma donation:

Permanent Disqualifications:

  • HIV/AIDS
  • Hepatitis B or C (chronic)
  • Certain blood disorders
  • History of illegal IV drug use
  • Human growth hormone recipients (before 1985)

Temporary Disqualifications:

  • Recent tattoos or piercings (3-12 months)
  • Pregnancy or recent pregnancy (6 weeks postpartum)
  • Recent illness or infection (varies by illness)
  • Certain medications (varies by medication)
  • Low protein or hematocrit levels (can retest)
  • Travel to malaria-risk areas (12 months)

The GCAM Plasma Donation Process

Understanding what to expect during your GCAM Plasma donation helps you prepare and ensures a smooth, efficient visit.

First-Time Donor Timeline

Your first visit to GCAM Plasma will take longer than subsequent visits. Plan for 2-3 hours total:

  1. Registration (20-30 minutes): Complete paperwork, provide documentation, create donor profile
  2. Medical History (15-20 minutes): Detailed questionnaire about health, medications, travel, lifestyle
  3. Physical Exam (15-20 minutes): Blood pressure, pulse, temperature, vein check, physical assessment
  4. Protein/Hematocrit Test (5-10 minutes): Finger-stick blood test to verify eligibility
  5. Donation (45-90 minutes): Actual plasma collection process
  6. Post-Donation (10-15 minutes): Recovery period, receive payment, schedule next visit

Return Donor Timeline

Subsequent GCAM Plasma visits are much faster, typically 60-90 minutes total:

  1. Check-in (5 minutes): Scan ID or use kiosk
  2. Health Screening (10 minutes): Vital signs, protein test
  3. Donation (45-60 minutes): Plasma collection
  4. Post-Donation (5-10 minutes): Recovery and payment

The Donation Process Explained

Here's exactly what happens during the plasma donation itself:

  1. Preparation: Technician cleans your arm and finds suitable vein
  2. Needle insertion: Sterile needle inserted (similar feel to blood donation)
  3. Blood draw cycle: Blood flows into machine (about 60 seconds)
  4. Separation cycle: Machine separates plasma from blood cells (about 90 seconds)
  5. Return cycle: Red blood cells returned to your body with saline (about 60 seconds)
  6. Repeat: Cycles repeat 4-6 times depending on volume collected
  7. Completion: Needle removed, bandage applied, observation period

The entire collection process takes 45-90 minutes depending on your weight, vein quality, and hydration level. Better hydration leads to faster flow and shorter donation times.

Preparing for Donation

Follow these preparation tips for the best experience:

Day Before:

  • Drink 64+ oz of water
  • Eat protein-rich foods (lean meat, eggs, beans)
  • Avoid fatty foods that can affect protein test
  • Get good sleep (7-8 hours)
  • Avoid alcohol

Day Of:

  • Drink 16-20 oz of water 1-2 hours before
  • Eat a healthy meal with protein
  • Avoid fatty foods (no fast food, fried foods)
  • Bring entertainment (phone, tablet, book)
  • Wear comfortable clothing with easy sleeve access
  • Bring required documents (first visit only)

After Donation:

  • Drink extra fluids (16-24 oz)
  • Avoid heavy lifting for 4-6 hours
  • Keep bandage on for at least 4 hours
  • Eat a good meal within 2 hours
  • Avoid alcohol for 24 hours
  • Rest if feeling lightheaded
