Can You Donate Plasma on Accutane?

No, you cannot donate plasma while taking Accutane (isotretinoin) in 2026. You must wait:

  • During treatment: Cannot donate
  • After stopping: Wait 1 month minimum
  • Reason: Birth defect risk
  • All forms: Accutane, Claravis, Absorica, etc.

Why Accutane Defers Donation

Isotretinoin causes severe birth defects:

  • Plasma may be given to pregnant women
  • Even small amounts are dangerous
  • Drug stays in system for weeks
  • 1 month clearance is safety margin

How It Works

  • Isotretinoin is a powerful retinoid
  • Highly teratogenic (causes birth defects)
  • Plasma products could contain traces
  • Recipients may include pregnant patients

Frequently Asked Questions

I finished Accutane 2 years ago - can I donate?

Yes! The 1-month waiting period is all that's required. After that, past Accutane use doesn't affect eligibility.

I'm on a low dose - does that change the wait time?

No. Any dose of isotretinoin requires the same 1-month waiting period after the last dose.

Can I donate the day after I stop Accutane?

No. You must wait the full 30 days. The drug stays in your system and slowly clears.

What about topical tretinoin (Retin-A)?

Topical retinoids like tretinoin, adapalene (Differin), and tazarotene don't disqualify you. Only oral isotretinoin (Accutane-type drugs) require a waiting period.
