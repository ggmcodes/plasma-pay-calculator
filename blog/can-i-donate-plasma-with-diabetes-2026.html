Type 1 Diabetes & Plasma Donation

General Guidelines

Type 1 diabetics face more restrictions because they require insulin:

  • Many centers: Do not accept Type 1 diabetics
  • Some centers: May accept if very well-controlled
  • Key factor: Insulin dependence is the main concern

Why the Restrictions?

  • Insulin use raises safety concerns during donation
  • Blood sugar fluctuations during the process
  • Risk of hypoglycemia after donation
Type 1 Tip

If you have Type 1 diabetes, call centers directly before visiting. Policies vary significantly. Some may accept you with a doctor's letter and recent A1C results.

Type 2 Diabetes & Plasma Donation

Good News for Type 2 Diabetics

Many Type 2 diabetics can donate plasma, especially if:

  • Blood sugar is well-controlled
  • A1C is in acceptable range (typically under 8-9%)
  • No serious complications
  • Not using bovine insulin

Insulin Users: Can You Donate?

The Insulin Question

Insulin use is the biggest factor affecting diabetic donor eligibility:

Insulin TypeEligibility
Bovine (beef) insulinPermanent deferral - all centers
Human/synthetic insulinVaries by center

Why Bovine Insulin Disqualifies

Bovine insulin is permanently disqualifying because of theoretical mad cow disease (vCJD) risk. This is FDA-mandated across all plasma centers.

Good News

Bovine insulin is rarely used today. Most modern insulin is human synthetic (recombinant). Check your insulin label or ask your pharmacist to confirm.

Tips for Diabetic Plasma Donors

Before Your First Visit

  1. Call the center - Explain your diabetes type and medications
  2. Know your A1C - Recent results may be required
  3. Get a doctor's letter - Some centers require physician clearance
  4. Bring medication list - Include all diabetes medications

Day of Donation

  • Check blood sugar before - Don't donate if too low or too high
  • Eat normally - Don't skip meals before donation
  • Bring snacks - Fast-acting carbs for after
  • Tell staff - Inform them you're diabetic
  • Monitor after - Watch for low blood sugar post-donation

Ongoing Donations

  • Maintain good control - High A1C may affect eligibility
  • Stay hydrated - Extra important for diabetics
  • Eat protein - Helps stabilize blood sugar
  • Listen to your body - Skip if blood sugar is unstable

Frequently Asked Questions

Will they test my blood sugar at the center?

Most centers don't routinely test blood sugar. They check other vitals like blood pressure and hemoglobin. However, if you disclose diabetes, they may ask about your control.

Can donating plasma affect my blood sugar?

Possibly. Some diabetics report blood sugar fluctuations after donation. Monitor your levels closely the first few times you donate.

Should I adjust my insulin on donation day?

Talk to your doctor. Don't make medication changes without medical advice. Generally, maintain your normal routine.

What if I'm pre-diabetic?

Pre-diabetes is usually not a barrier to plasma donation. You should still disclose it during screening.
