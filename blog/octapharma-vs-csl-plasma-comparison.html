Frequently Asked Questions

Does Octapharma or CSL Plasma pay more in 2026?

CSL Plasma generally pays more with rates of $50-100 per donation compared to Octapharma's $50-75. CSL also offers higher new donor bonuses (up to $1,000 vs $700). However, Octapharma's OctaRewards program provides consistent loyalty bonuses that can close the gap for regular donors.

What is the OctaRewards program vs CSL iGive Rewards?

OctaRewards offers e-gift card incentives and milestone bonuses for consistent donations. CSL's iGive Rewards focuses on referral bonuses ($100 per referral) and occasional promotional bonuses. OctaRewards is better for consistent donors while iGive is better for those who can refer friends.

Which has better locations - Octapharma or CSL Plasma?

CSL Plasma has significantly more locations with 300+ centers in 45 states compared to Octapharma's 150+ locations in 35 states. CSL offers better nationwide coverage especially in rural areas, while Octapharma focuses on urban and suburban markets.

How do wait times compare between Octapharma and CSL?

Octapharma generally has shorter wait times averaging 30-60 minutes for return donors compared to CSL's 45-90 minutes. Octapharma centers tend to be less crowded due to fewer locations, resulting in a smoother donation experience.

Which is better for first-time plasma donors?

CSL Plasma is generally better for first-time donors due to higher new donor bonuses (up to $1,000 vs $700) and more locations. However, if an Octapharma center is more convenient and has current promotions, it can be a good choice for the better overall experience.
