Can You Donate Plasma With Bursitis?

Yes, bursitis doesn't prevent donation in 2026:

  • Joint inflammation: Not a deferral
  • Location varies: Hip, shoulder, knee, elbow
  • Pain meds: Most acceptable
  • Comfort: Main consideration

Why Bursitis Doesn't Disqualify

Key points:

  • Localized issue: Bursa inflammation
  • Not systemic: Doesn't affect blood
  • Not contagious: Cannot transmit
  • Common condition: Many donors have it

Elbow Bursitis (Olecranon)

Special consideration:

  • Swelling at elbow: Back of elbow, not draw site
  • Draw site: Inner elbow (antecubital)
  • Usually OK: Different area
  • If severe: Use other arm

Comfort During Donation

Depending on location:

  • Hip bursitis: Reclining position may be fine
  • Shoulder bursitis: Keep arm relaxed
  • Knee bursitis: Legs elevated in chair
  • Request pillows: Extra support if needed

Tips for Bursitis Donors

  1. Time pain meds: Before if needed
  2. Communicate: Tell staff your condition
  3. Use good arm: If elbow bursitis
  4. Post-injection wait: 24-48 hours
  5. Comfort first: Don't push through pain

Frequently Asked Questions

I have hip bursitis - will lying in the donation chair hurt?

The donation chair is typically well-padded and semi-reclined, which most people with hip bursitis find comfortable. You can request extra padding if needed. The position is usually easier than sitting in a regular chair.

I have elbow bursitis (olecranon) - can I still donate?

Yes, olecranon bursitis affects the back of your elbow, while plasma draws happen at the inner elbow. They're different areas. If your elbow is very swollen or painful, you can use your other arm for the draw.

I take ibuprofen daily for bursitis - is that OK?

Yes, NSAIDs like ibuprofen, naproxen, or aspirin are acceptable for plasma donation. You can take your pain medication as usual before donating.

I just had a cortisone shot for bursitis - when can I donate?

Wait 24-48 hours after a cortisone injection before donating plasma. After this brief wait, you can resume normal donation. If the injection was near your draw arm's elbow, consider using the other arm.

Will extending my arm during donation make bursitis worse?

The extended arm position for donation shouldn't significantly aggravate bursitis in most cases. Shoulder bursitis donors can keep the arm relaxed and supported. Elbow bursitis donors can use their less affected arm.

Joint Pain OK

Bursitis doesn't prevent plasma donation! Whether you have hip, shoulder, knee, or elbow bursitis, you can typically donate. Most pain treatments are acceptable. Just wait 24-48 hours after cortisone injections and find a comfortable position!
