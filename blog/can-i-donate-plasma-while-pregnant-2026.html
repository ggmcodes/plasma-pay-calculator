During Pregnancy

Why You Cannot Donate While Pregnant

All plasma centers prohibit donation during pregnancy for several important reasons:

  • Iron needs: Your body needs extra iron for the baby
  • Blood volume: Pregnancy already increases blood volume demands
  • Protein requirements: Plasma proteins are needed for fetal development
  • Maternal health: Donation stress could affect pregnancy
  • Antibody development: Pregnancy creates unique antibodies
Universal Rule

No plasma center in the US accepts pregnant donors. This is a safety requirement across BioLife, CSL, Octapharma, Grifols, and all other centers.

Requirements to Resume

Considerations for Breastfeeding Donors

  • Stay extra hydrated: Breastfeeding + donation = high fluid needs
  • Monitor milk supply: Some women report temporary decrease
  • Eat extra protein: Your body needs more nutrients
  • Watch for fatigue: Both activities are demanding
Hydration Tip

If breastfeeding and donating, drink at least 80-100 oz of water daily. Pump before your appointment if needed, and bring water to sip during donation.

Tips for New Moms Returning to Donation

  1. Wait the full 6 weeks - Don't rush, your body needs recovery time
  2. Check hemoglobin first - Postpartum anemia is common
  3. Start with one donation - See how your body responds
  4. Bring snacks - You'll need extra calories
  5. Stay hydrated - Especially if breastfeeding
  6. Arrange childcare - First donation may take 2-3 hours

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I didn't know I was pregnant and donated?

Inform the center immediately. A single donation during early pregnancy is unlikely to cause harm, but the center needs to know for your safety and records.

Can donating plasma affect fertility?

There's no evidence that plasma donation affects fertility. You can donate while trying to conceive. However, once pregnant, you must stop immediately.

Will plasma donation affect my breast milk?

Most mothers report no issues. Some notice temporary dips in supply immediately after donation. Stay very hydrated to minimize any effects.

I had a C-section - is the wait longer?

Some centers require 3 months after C-section instead of 6 weeks. This allows for proper surgical healing. Check with your specific center.

What if I'm a gestational surrogate?

Same rules apply - no donation during pregnancy, standard postpartum waiting period. Disclose surrogacy during screening.
