Quick Answer

Most centers require blood pressure between 90/50 and 180/100 mmHg. To avoid deferral: sit quietly before screening, avoid caffeine, practice deep breathing, and stay calm. BP deferrals are same-day only - you can return when readings normalize.

Blood Pressure Requirements

Acceptable Ranges

MeasurementMinimumMaximum
Systolic (top number)90 mmHg180 mmHg
Diastolic (bottom number)50 mmHg100 mmHg

Center Variations

  • Some centers use 160/100 as upper limit
  • Requirements may vary slightly
  • Both numbers must be within range
  • Check your specific center's policy

What the Numbers Mean

  • Systolic (top): Pressure when heart beats
  • Diastolic (bottom): Pressure between beats
  • Example: 120/80 = 120 systolic, 80 diastolic

Day-Before Factors

  • High sodium: Can elevate BP next day
  • Poor sleep: Increases blood pressure
  • Alcohol: Next-day BP elevation
  • Stress: Carries over to appointment

Low Blood Pressure Deferrals

Causes of Low Readings

  • Dehydration: Reduces blood volume
  • Skipping meals: Can lower BP
  • Medications: Some lower BP significantly
  • Natural tendency: Some people run low

How to Raise BP Safely

  • Drink water and eat before appointment
  • Add a little salt to your pre-donation meal
  • Stand and move around before screening
  • Ensure adequate sleep

How to Lower Blood Pressure Before Donation

Immediate Techniques (At the Center)

5-Minute BP Reduction

  1. Sit comfortably with feet flat on floor
  2. Close your eyes or look down
  3. Breathe slowly: 4 seconds in, 6 seconds out
  4. Relax your shoulders and jaw
  5. Think of a calm, peaceful place
  6. Continue for 5-10 minutes

Day-Before Preparation

  • Limit sodium: Avoid salty foods
  • No alcohol: Skip drinking the night before
  • Get sleep: 7-8 hours reduces BP
  • Light exercise: Walking can help
  • Reduce stress: Avoid stressful situations

Morning of Donation

  • Skip or limit coffee/caffeine
  • Eat a balanced, low-sodium meal
  • Leave early to avoid rushing
  • Use restroom before screening
  • Sit quietly for 10-15 minutes before check

Long-Term BP Management

  • Regular exercise (30 min most days)
  • Maintain healthy weight
  • Limit sodium to 2,300mg daily
  • Manage stress
  • Take medications as prescribed

Blood Pressure Medications

Can You Donate on BP Medication?

Yes, in most cases:

  • Condition must be controlled
  • Reading must be within acceptable range
  • Must be stable on medication (not newly started)
  • Disclose all medications during screening

Common Acceptable BP Medications

  • ACE inhibitors (lisinopril, enalapril)
  • ARBs (losartan, valsartan)
  • Beta blockers (metoprolol, atenolol)
  • Calcium channel blockers (amlodipine)
  • Diuretics (hydrochlorothiazide)

Important Notes

  • Take medication as prescribed
  • Don't skip doses before donation
  • Some newer medications may have waiting periods
  • Check with center if uncertain

Frequently Asked Questions

What blood pressure is required for plasma donation?

Most centers require blood pressure between 90/50 and 180/100 mmHg. Some centers have stricter limits around 160/100 for the upper range. Both systolic (top) and diastolic (bottom) must be within range.

How can I lower my blood pressure before plasma donation?

Sit quietly for 10-15 minutes before screening, practice slow deep breathing, avoid caffeine for several hours before, limit salt intake the day before, and stay calm during the reading.

Why is my blood pressure high at the plasma center?

White coat syndrome (anxiety from medical settings), caffeine, stress, rushing to the appointment, full bladder, or talking during the reading can all elevate blood pressure temporarily.

Can I donate plasma if I take blood pressure medication?

Yes, many donors take BP medication. As long as your condition is controlled and your reading falls within acceptable range, you can usually donate. Disclose all medications during screening.

How long is a blood pressure deferral for plasma?

High blood pressure deferrals are typically same-day only. You can return another day when your BP is within range. If readings are consistently high, see a doctor before continuing.

What causes low blood pressure deferral for plasma?

Dehydration, skipping meals, certain medications, or naturally low BP can cause readings below 90/50. Hydrating and eating before your appointment usually resolves this.
