What Happens During Your First Visit

First visits take 2-4 hours. Here's the full process:

1

Check-In & Document Verification (15-30 min)

Staff verifies your ID, SSN, and proof of address. You'll fill out initial paperwork and create your donor profile.

2

Health History Questionnaire (20-40 min)

Answer questions about your medical history, medications, travel, lifestyle. Be honest‚Äîthey're checking for safety, not judging.

3

Physical Exam (15-30 min)

A brief physical: weight, blood pressure, pulse, temperature. They'll also check your arms for suitable veins.

4

Screening Tests (10-20 min)

Finger prick for protein and hematocrit levels. First visits also include additional blood tests sent to a lab.

5

The Actual Donation (45-90 min)

If approved, you'll donate! Blood is drawn, plasma separated, and red cells returned. You'll watch TV or use your phone.

6

Get Paid! (5 min)

Payment loaded to a prepaid debit card. First visit typically pays $100-150 with new donor bonuses.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I forgot one of my documents? ‚ñº

Unfortunately, you'll need all three documents (ID, SSN card, proof of address) to complete registration. If you forgot one, you'll need to leave and return when you have it. Some centers may let you hold your spot in line if you live nearby and can retrieve the document quickly.

Can I reschedule if I'm running late? ‚ñº

Many centers accept walk-ins, so there's often no formal appointment to reschedule. If you made an appointment, call ahead‚Äîsome centers are flexible. Keep in mind that first-time visits take 2-4 hours, so arriving late may mean not finishing before the center closes.

What if I fail the screening on my first visit? ‚ñº

You can typically come back another day once the issue is corrected. Common first-visit fails include dehydration (low protein) or nervousness (high BP/pulse). Prepare better and try again‚Äîyou don't lose your new donor bonus eligibility for failing a screening.
