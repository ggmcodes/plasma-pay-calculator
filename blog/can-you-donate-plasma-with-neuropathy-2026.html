Can You Donate Plasma With Neuropathy?

It depends on the cause in 2026:

  • Neuropathy itself: Not disqualifying
  • Underlying cause: May matter
  • Medications: Most acceptable
  • Comfort: Consideration for donation

Why Neuropathy Alone Doesn't Disqualify

Key points:

  • Plasma quality: Usually unaffected
  • Many donors have it: Very common

Frequently Asked Questions

I have peripheral neuropathy - can I donate plasma?

Usually yes, if the underlying cause doesn't disqualify you. Neuropathy itself (nerve damage) isn't a deferral. The key factors are what's causing it (diabetes, vitamin deficiency, etc.) and whether that condition affects eligibility.

I have diabetic neuropathy - can I still donate?

It depends on your diabetes management. Well-controlled type 2 diabetes typically doesn't prevent donation. Insulin-dependent diabetes may have restrictions. The neuropathy itself isn't the issue - it's the diabetes management that matters.

I take gabapentin for neuropathy - is that OK?

Yes, gabapentin (Neurontin) is acceptable for plasma donation. It's one of the most commonly prescribed neuropathy medications. Pregabalin (Lyrica), duloxetine (Cymbalta), and amitriptyline are also typically fine.

Will I feel the needle with neuropathy in my arms?

If you have numbness in your arms, you may feel the needle less than others. Let the staff know about your condition. They can ensure proper placement and monitor for any issues during donation.

I had chemo-induced neuropathy - can I donate?

The neuropathy itself isn't the issue, but your cancer history may affect eligibility. Most plasma centers have specific guidelines about cancer survivors - typically requiring a certain period of remission. Discuss your specific situation during screening.

Underlying Cause Matters

Neuropathy itself doesn't prevent plasma donation - it's the underlying cause that may affect eligibility. Most neuropathy medications are acceptable. If you have diabetic neuropathy, focus on diabetes management. Let staff know about any numbness in your arms!
