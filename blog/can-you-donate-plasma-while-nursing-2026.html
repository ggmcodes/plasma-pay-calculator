Can You Donate Plasma While Breastfeeding?

Usually no - most centers defer breastfeeding mothers in 2026:

  • Most centers: Wait 6 weeks after fully weaning
  • Some centers: May allow if baby is 6+ months old
  • Hydration concerns: Nursing already demands fluids
  • Milk supply: Donation could affect production

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm only nursing once a day - can I donate?

Some centers may allow donation if you're barely nursing (once daily or less) and baby is older (6+ months). Call your specific center to ask. Full deferral is most common, but policies vary.

Will donating plasma affect my breast milk quality?

This isn't well studied. Theoretically, temporary changes in protein or hydration could affect milk. Most centers defer nursing mothers partly to avoid this unknown risk.

I stopped nursing yesterday - when can I donate?

Most centers require 6 weeks after completely weaning. This allows your body to fully transition out of milk production and recover normal fluid/protein balance.

Can I pump and dump after donating?

This isn't the concern - plasma donation doesn't put anything harmful in your milk. The issue is the physical demands on your body affecting milk production and your recovery.

I need the money - is there any way to donate while nursing?

Some centers may have exceptions. Call multiple centers and explain your situation. If baby is older and nursing minimally, you might find one that allows it. But most will defer.

Baby First

While the extra income from plasma donation is appealing, your baby's nutrition and your health come first. Most centers want you to wait until you've finished breastfeeding. Once weaned and recovered, you can maximize your donation schedule!
