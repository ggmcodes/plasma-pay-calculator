Quick Answer

WAIT

Until infection clears and antibiotics complete

Can You Donate Plasma With a UTI?

No, wait until the infection is cleared in 2026:

  • Active UTI: Cannot donate
  • On antibiotics: Wait until course completed
  • After treatment: Can donate once resolved
  • Quick turnaround: Usually only 3-7 day wait

Why UTIs Disqualify Temporarily

Active infection concerns:

  • Bacteria present: Active infection in your system
  • Antibiotics: Treatment needs to complete
  • Feeling unwell: Discomfort affects donation
  • Fever possible: Some UTIs cause fever

Common UTI Antibiotics

All require completing course:

  • Macrobid (nitrofurantoin): 5-7 day course
  • Bactrim: 3 day course common
  • Cipro: 3-7 days depending on severity
  • Keflex: 7-14 days for complicated UTI

Signs You're Ready

Before donating again:

  • Antibiotics finished: Completed full course
  • No symptoms: No burning, urgency, frequency
  • No fever: Temperature normal
  • Feeling well: Back to normal

Returning to Donation

  1. Finish all antibiotics: Even if feeling better
  2. Wait 24-48 hours: After last dose
  3. Symptoms gone: No discomfort remaining
  4. Extra hydration: Important for UTI recovery and donation
  5. Mention recent infection: If asked about antibiotics

Chronic/Recurrent UTIs

If you get frequent UTIs:

  • Between infections: Can donate normally
  • Preventive antibiotics: May need to discuss with center
  • Pattern: Know your trigger times
  • Hydration helps: Both for UTI prevention and donation

Practical Considerations

Donating with UTI history:

  • Sitting 45-90 min: May be uncomfortable during active UTI
  • Bathroom access: Limited during donation
  • Hydration required: Drink more for both
  • Short deferral: Only a few days typically

Frequently Asked Questions

I started antibiotics for a UTI yesterday - when can I donate?

Wait until you complete your full antibiotic course (usually 3-7 days) plus 24-48 hours after the last dose. So if you're on a 3-day course, you could potentially donate in 4-5 days if you're feeling completely better.

My UTI symptoms are gone but I have 2 days of antibiotics left - can I donate now?

No, you should complete your full antibiotic course first. Even though you feel better, the infection may not be fully cleared. Stopping antibiotics early can lead to recurrence and antibiotic resistance. Finish the course, then donate.

I get UTIs frequently - will this affect my ability to donate long-term?

Recurrent UTIs don't permanently disqualify you. You just need to wait out each infection and antibiotic course. Between infections, you can donate normally. If you're on a preventive antibiotic regimen, discuss with your plasma center.

Do I need to tell the plasma center I had a UTI?

If the screening questionnaire asks about recent infections or antibiotics (most do), answer honestly. Mention the UTI and when you finished antibiotics. If it's been resolved for several days, it typically won't be an issue.

Can a UTI affect my plasma test results?

An active infection can potentially affect some blood tests, which is another reason to wait until you're recovered. Once the infection is cleared and you've finished antibiotics, your results should be normal.

Quick Recovery

UTIs are temporary deferrals that usually only last 3-7 days. Complete your antibiotic course, wait until symptoms fully resolve, and you can return to donating. The good news is UTIs are common and easily treated - you won't be out of commission for long!
