Can You Donate Plasma With Gout?

Yes, gout generally doesn't disqualify you from plasma donation in 2026:

  • Between flares: Usually can donate
  • During flare: Wait until resolved
  • Most medications OK: Common gout drugs acceptable
  • Not autoimmune: Different from RA or lupus

Why Gout Usually Doesn't Disqualify

  • Not autoimmune: Metabolic condition, not immune disorder
  • Doesn't affect blood: Uric acid issue, not plasma issue
  • Medications OK: Most gout drugs aren't immunosuppressants
  • Localized: Joint inflammation, not systemic

Tips for Gout Patients

  1. Stay hydrated: Extra important for gout AND plasma donation
  2. Don't donate during flares: Wait until resolved
  3. Disclose medications: Let center know what you take
  4. Comfortable seating: Ask for foot rest if needed
  5. Diet watch: Avoid triggers before donation

Hydration Importance

Hydration matters for both gout and plasma:

  • Dehydration can trigger gout flares
  • Plasma donation requires good hydration
  • Drink extra water day before and day of
  • Water helps flush uric acid

Frequently Asked Questions

I'm having a gout flare right now - should I cancel my appointment?

Yes, it's best to wait until your flare resolves. You'll be more comfortable, and acute inflammation can affect your vitals. Reschedule for when you're feeling better.

Will plasma donation trigger a gout flare?

Plasma donation itself doesn't typically trigger gout. However, dehydration can, so drink plenty of water before and after donation to stay safe.

I take allopurinol every day - is that a problem?

No, daily allopurinol for uric acid control is generally acceptable for plasma donation. It's a common, well-tolerated medication that doesn't affect plasma quality.

Good News

Gout is one of the conditions that usually doesn't prevent plasma donation. Just make sure you're between flares and staying well-hydrated!
