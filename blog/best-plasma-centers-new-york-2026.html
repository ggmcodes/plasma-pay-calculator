NYC Plasma Donation Requirements

What You Need to Donate in NYC

  • ✓Age 18-69 years old
  • ✓Weigh at least 110 lbs
  • ✓Valid photo ID (NY ID, driver's license, passport)
  • ✓Proof of current address (utility bill, bank statement dated within 30-60 days)
  • ✓Social Security card or proof of legal residency
  • ✓Pass health screening (vitals, hematocrit test)

NYC Tip: If you recently moved, bring multiple forms of address proof. NYC landlords often don't put tenants on utility bills, so bring bank statements, official mail, or your lease.

NYC Donor Tips: Maximize Earnings

  • 1
    Seriously Consider NJ

    New Jersey centers are accessible via PATH train from Manhattan and often offer better pay and shorter wait times than NYC locations. The 20-minute ride to Newark or Jersey City can be worth $10+ more per visit.

  • 2
    Use Off-Peak Transit

    Schedule donations during off-peak subway hours (10am-3pm or after 8pm) to avoid crowds and get seats. Standing on a packed subway after donating is uncomfortable.

  • 3
    Book Appointments Early

    NYC centers get crowded, especially on weekends. Book appointments 1-2 weeks ahead for preferred time slots. Early morning weekday slots have the shortest waits.

  • 4
    Budget for Transit Costs

    Factor in $5.80 round-trip subway or $6.00 PATH fare. You're still earning well, but choose a center that maximizes your net pay after transit costs.

  • 5
    Eat Before You Go

    NYC has expensive food options near most centers. Eat at home before donating to avoid overspending on post-donation snacks.

  • 6
    Download Center Apps

    BioLife and CSL have great apps showing wait times, bonus offers, and appointment scheduling. Check before you leave to avoid wasted trips.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are there so few plasma centers in Manhattan?

Manhattan's extremely high real estate costs make it difficult for plasma centers to operate profitably. Centers need significant floor space for equipment and donor beds, which is prohibitively expensive in Manhattan. Most NYC plasma centers are in the outer boroughs where rent is more reasonable.

Should I donate in NYC or New Jersey?

If you're in Manhattan or western Brooklyn, NJ centers via PATH are often your best bet. They frequently offer better bonuses, shorter wait times, and the 20-30 minute PATH ride is comparable to subway travel to outer borough centers.

How often can I donate plasma in New York?

You can donate up to twice per week with at least one day between donations (e.g., Monday and Wednesday, or Tuesday and Thursday). Most regular donors do 6-8 donations per month.

Is plasma income taxable in NY?

Yes, plasma donation payments are considered taxable income by the IRS and New York State. Centers issue a 1099 if you earn over $600. Keep records of your earnings for tax purposes.

Can I donate if I'm a college student in NYC?

Yes! You'll need proof of your current address (dorm mail or utility bill works) and valid ID. Many NYC students donate plasma for extra income. Check for centers near your campus.

What's the best day and time to donate in NYC?

Early morning on weekdays (7-9 AM) typically has the shortest waits. Avoid weekends and late afternoons, which are busiest. Mid-week (Tuesday-Thursday) tends to be less crowded than Monday or Friday.

Can I switch between NYC and NJ plasma centers?

You can donate at different companies, but you can only be registered at one center per company at a time. You cannot donate at two different companies in the same week.
