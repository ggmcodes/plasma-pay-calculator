Can You Donate Plasma While Pregnant?

No, you cannot donate plasma while pregnant in 2026. This is a universal rule at all plasma centers for safety reasons:

  • All trimesters: Not allowed at any stage
  • All centers: Universal policy
  • Safety first: Protects mother and baby
  • Resume later: Can donate after recovery

Why Pregnancy Disqualifies Donation

The restriction exists for important medical reasons:

  • Blood volume: Your body needs extra blood for baby
  • Nutrition: Proteins and nutrients needed for fetus
  • Antibodies: Pregnancy creates antibodies that can affect plasma
  • TRALI risk: Transfusion-related acute lung injury concern
  • Dehydration: Risk to both mother and baby

The HLA Antibody Issue

Pregnancy creates HLA antibodies that can:

  • Cause reactions in plasma recipients
  • Lead to TRALI in transfusion patients
  • Persist for years after pregnancy

After Miscarriage or Termination

If pregnancy ends early:

  • Waiting period: Usually 6 weeks
  • Medical clearance: Full recovery required
  • No bleeding: Must have stopped
  • Check with center: Policies vary

Returning to Plasma Donation

When you're ready to donate again:

  1. Wait required time: At least 6 weeks postpartum
  2. Full recovery: Physically healed
  3. Normal energy: Not overly fatigued
  4. Breastfeeding status: Check center policy
  5. Meet weight requirement: Still need 110+ lbs

Breastfeeding and Plasma Donation

Policies on breastfeeding vary:

  • Some centers: Allow donation while breastfeeding
  • Other centers: Require you to stop first
  • Reasoning: Hydration and nutrition concerns
  • Ask your center: Get their specific policy

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I didn't know I was pregnant?

If pregnancy is discovered during screening, you'll be deferred. Any donations given while unknowingly pregnant may be discarded for safety.

Can donating plasma affect fertility?

Regular plasma donation does not affect fertility. However, you should stop donating when trying to conceive or as soon as you know you're pregnant.

Will I get my new donor bonus again after pregnancy?

Usually no - you're considered a returning donor. But some centers offer comeback bonuses for lapsed donors.
