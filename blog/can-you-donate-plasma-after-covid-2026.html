Can You Donate Plasma After Having COVID?

Yes, you can donate plasma after COVID-19 in 2026:

  • Wait until recovered: Symptom-free required
  • Waiting period: Typically 7-14 days after recovery
  • No fever: Must be fever-free without medication
  • Feel well: Normal energy levels returned

Requirements to Donate Post-COVID

You must meet all criteria:

  1. Symptom-free: No cough, congestion, body aches
  2. No fever: Temperature normal for 24+ hours without medication
  3. Feel well: Energy levels back to normal
  4. Adequate time: Waited required period
  5. Meet regular criteria: All other eligibility requirements

Long COVID Considerations

If you have persistent symptoms:

  • Fatigue: If still fatigued, may not be ready
  • Shortness of breath: Must be resolved
  • Heart issues: Palpitations or chest pain - wait
  • Brain fog: Evaluate your overall wellness

When Long COVID May Affect Eligibility

  • Ongoing symptoms: Active symptoms may defer
  • Heart complications: Myocarditis history - may permanently defer
  • Blood clotting issues: Need to be resolved
  • Autoimmune responses: May need evaluation

COVID Vaccines and Boosters

2026 vaccine rules are simpler:

  • Pfizer/Moderna: Usually no waiting period
  • Other vaccines: Check with your center
  • Feeling well: No side effects on donation day
  • No fever: Must be fever-free

Evolution of COVID Rules

Rules have relaxed since 2020-2021:

  • 2020-2021: Strict 28+ day deferrals common
  • 2022-2023: Shortened to 10-14 days for most
  • 2024-2026: Most centers use 7-10 day standard
  • 2026: Treated similar to other respiratory infections

Tips for Post-COVID Donors

  1. Wait fully: Don't rush back before you're ready
  2. Extra hydration: COVID can affect hydration status
  3. Rest well: Make sure energy is back to normal
  4. Be honest: Disclose recent illness during screening
  5. First donation easy: Consider shorter donation if feeling tired

Frequently Asked Questions

I tested positive but never had symptoms - when can I donate?

Asymptomatic cases typically require waiting 7-10 days from your positive test date. You should still feel completely well on donation day.

I had COVID months ago but still have some fatigue - can I donate?

If you're experiencing ongoing fatigue or other long COVID symptoms, you may want to wait until you feel fully recovered. Plasma donation can temporarily increase fatigue.

Does having had COVID affect my plasma quality?

No, having recovered from COVID doesn't affect your plasma quality. In fact, recovered patients may have antibodies that could be beneficial.

2026 Update

COVID policies have normalized. Most centers treat COVID similar to other respiratory infections - wait until recovered and symptom-free.
