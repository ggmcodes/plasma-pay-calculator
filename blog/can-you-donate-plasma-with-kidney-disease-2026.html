Can You Donate Plasma With Kidney Disease?

Generally no - kidney disease disqualifies you from plasma donation in 2026:

  • CKD: Chronic kidney disease defers most stages
  • Dialysis: Permanent deferral
  • Transplant: Cannot donate
  • Kidney failure: Not eligible

Why Kidney Disease Disqualifies

Kidneys and plasma donation don't mix well:

  • Protein processing: Kidneys filter plasma proteins - donation adds stress
  • Fluid balance: CKD affects fluid management
  • Toxin clearance: Compromised kidneys can't handle additional stress
  • Medication interactions: Many kidney patients take immunosuppressants

Dialysis Patients

If you're on dialysis, you cannot donate plasma:

  • Hemodialysis: Permanent deferral
  • Peritoneal dialysis: Permanent deferral
  • Blood volume concerns: Already processing blood externally
  • Infection risk: Higher vulnerability

Kidney Transplant Recipients

Transplant recipients cannot donate:

  • Immunosuppressants: Required medications disqualify
  • Rejection monitoring: Body under stress
  • Infection risk: Compromised immune system
  • Single kidney function: Protecting transplanted organ

Frequently Asked Questions

I had a kidney infection years ago but I'm fine now - can I donate?

A resolved kidney infection (UTI that spread to kidneys) may not permanently disqualify you. If your kidney function is normal now and you have no chronic issues, you may be eligible. Be honest during screening.

My eGFR is borderline (60-70) - can I try?

Borderline kidney function typically disqualifies you. Plasma donation stresses the kidneys, and centers don't want to risk worsening your condition.

I donated before my kidney disease diagnosis - is that a problem?

Previous donations before diagnosis aren't a concern. Once you're diagnosed with CKD or kidney disease, you should stop donating and inform the center of your diagnosis.

Important

Kidney disease is a serious condition. Prioritize your health and follow your nephrologist's recommendations over any desire to donate plasma.
