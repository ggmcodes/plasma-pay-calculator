Can You Donate Plasma With Herpes?

Yes, most people with herpes can donate plasma - as long as they're not experiencing an active outbreak. Both oral herpes (HSV-1) and genital herpes (HSV-2) follow the same general rule.

The General Rule

  • No active outbreak: Usually eligible
  • Active lesions: Cannot donate until healed
  • On medication: Usually acceptable
  • Dormant virus: Not a barrier

HSV-1 (Oral Herpes / Cold Sores)

You CAN Donate If:

  • No active cold sore: Lips are clear
  • Sore has fully healed: No scabs or lesions
  • Feeling healthy: No prodrome symptoms
  • Taking suppressive therapy: Valtrex, acyclovir OK

You CANNOT Donate If:

  • Active cold sore: Visible lesion
  • Tingling/burning: Prodrome symptoms starting
  • Healing sore: Still has scab

HSV-1 Facts

Approximately 50-80% of US adults have HSV-1. It's extremely common and not a permanent deferral.

HSV-2 (Genital Herpes)

You CAN Donate If:

  • No active outbreak: No lesions present
  • Virus dormant: No symptoms
  • On suppressive therapy: Daily medication OK
  • Otherwise healthy: No other issues

You CANNOT Donate If:

  • Active outbreak: Visible lesions
  • Prodrome symptoms: Tingling, burning, itching
  • Recently diagnosed: May need to wait for stability

During Outbreaks

Why You Can't Donate During Outbreaks

  • Viral shedding: Virus more active in blood
  • Open lesions: Infection risk
  • Staff safety: Protects healthcare workers
  • Plasma quality: May affect plasma

When to Return After Outbreak

  • Cold sores: When fully healed, no scab
  • Genital outbreak: When lesions completely healed
  • Generally: 48-72 hours after healing

Donor Tips for HSV Positive Donors

Before Donating

  1. Check your body: No active outbreaks
  2. Take medications: As prescribed
  3. Know your triggers: Avoid before donation
  4. Be honest: Disclose if having outbreak

During Screening

  • Disclose diagnosis: If asked directly
  • Report symptoms: Any tingling or prodrome
  • List medications: Antivirals you take

Managing Outbreaks

  • Track patterns: Know when outbreaks occur
  • Schedule around: Donate when stable
  • Suppressive therapy: Reduces outbreak frequency

Common Questions

Do I have to tell them I have herpes?

You should answer honestly if asked about current symptoms or outbreaks. The dormant virus itself is not typically a screening question.

Will they test for herpes?

Standard plasma screening tests don't typically include HSV testing. They test for HIV, hepatitis, and other blood-borne pathogens.

Can herpes spread through plasma donation?

No - the plasma collection process is sterile, and manufacturing processes inactivate viruses.
