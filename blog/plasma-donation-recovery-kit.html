Wound Care & Bruise Prevention

Proper care of the donation site helps it heal faster and prevents visible bruising.

Preventing and Treating Bruises

Some bruising is normal after plasma donation, but you can minimize it with proper care: keep firm pressure on the site for 3-5 minutes after needle removal, wear a compression sleeve for several hours, and apply arnica cream after removing the bandage (usually 4-6 hours post-donation).

Recovery Timeline: When to Use Each Product

0h

Immediately After Donation

Stay at the center for 15 minutes. Drink the juice/snack they provide. Apply firm pressure to the bandage. Don't remove the bandage yet.

15m

Leaving the Center

Put on your compression sleeve over the bandage. Drink a Liquid I.V. mixed in your water bottle. Eat an RXBAR or drink a Core Power shake.

4h

4-6 Hours Post-Donation

Remove the bandage (unless still bleeding). Apply arnica gel to the puncture site. Keep wearing the compression sleeve. Continue hydrating.

8h

Evening / Before Bed

Apply arnica gel again. Consider wearing compression sleeve overnight or replacing with self-adhesive bandage. Drink another electrolyte mix. Take iron supplement with food (if using).

24h

Next Day

Continue applying arnica gel 2-3x daily. Eat high-protein meals (or use protein powder). Stay well-hydrated. Site should be healing well.

48h

Two Days Later

Plasma levels should be fully regenerated. Any bruising should be fading. You're ready for your next donation (per center schedule).

Frequently Asked Questions

What should be in a plasma donation recovery kit?

A complete plasma donation recovery kit should include: electrolyte drink mix for rapid rehydration, protein supplements or bars, compression arm sleeve for the donation site, arnica cream for bruise prevention, iron supplements (if recommended by your doctor), and a quality water bottle.

How long does it take to recover from plasma donation?

Most donors feel normal within 24-48 hours after plasma donation. Full plasma regeneration takes about 48-72 hours. Having proper hydration and protein can speed recovery. The donation site typically heals within 1-2 days.

What helps plasma donation bruises heal faster?

To help bruises heal faster: apply pressure immediately after the needle is removed, wear a compression sleeve for several hours, use arnica cream after the bandage is removed, stay hydrated, and avoid heavy lifting with that arm for 24 hours.

Should I rest after donating plasma?

Light rest is recommended for 15-30 minutes immediately after donation. Avoid strenuous exercise and heavy lifting for 24 hours. Normal daily activities are fine. Stay hydrated and eat protein-rich foods to support recovery.

Can I exercise after donating plasma?

Light activity is fine the same day, but avoid strenuous exercise, heavy lifting, or intense workouts for at least 24 hours. This allows your body to recover and prevents complications at the donation site.
