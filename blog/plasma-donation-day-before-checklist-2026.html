Quick Answer

The day before donation: drink 8-10 glasses of water, eat iron-rich foods and protein at every meal, avoid alcohol and excessive caffeine, get 7-8 hours of sleep, and skip intense exercise. This preparation significantly improves your chances of passing screening.

Morning Preparation (Day Before)

Breakfast Goals

  • Include protein: Eggs, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese
  • Add iron: Fortified cereal, spinach in eggs
  • Start hydrating: 2 glasses of water with breakfast
  • Limit caffeine: 1 cup of coffee is fine

Morning Hydration

  • Drink 16-24 oz water before lunch
  • Herbal tea counts toward hydration
  • Avoid energy drinks

Afternoon Preparation

Lunch Goals

  • Protein-focused: Chicken, fish, beans, or lentils
  • Complex carbs: Whole grains, brown rice, quinoa
  • Vegetables: Leafy greens for iron
  • Avoid: Fast food, fried foods, heavy creamy sauces

Afternoon Hydration

  • Continue drinking water throughout the day
  • Aim for 2-3 more glasses by dinner
  • Watch for signs of dehydration: dark urine, thirst

Activity Considerations

  • Light exercise OK: Walking, gentle yoga, stretching
  • Avoid: Heavy lifting, intense cardio, hot yoga
  • Rest if tired: Don't push yourself

Evening Preparation

Dinner: The Most Important Meal

Your dinner the night before directly affects your screening results.

  • Option 1: Beef stir-fry with vegetables and brown rice
  • Option 2: Grilled chicken with spinach salad and baked potato
  • Option 3: Salmon with quinoa and roasted vegetables
  • Option 4: Bean chili with cornbread (vegetarian)
  • Option 5: Lean burger with side salad and sweet potato fries

Evening Hydration

  • 2-3 more glasses of water with and after dinner
  • Stop drinking 2 hours before bed to avoid disrupted sleep
  • Total for day: 8-10 glasses minimum

Bedtime Routine

  • Target: 7-8 hours of sleep
  • Avoid screens: 30 minutes before bed
  • Set alarm: Allow time for breakfast tomorrow
  • Prepare: Lay out ID, center card, comfortable clothes

What to Avoid the Day Before

Foods to Skip

  • High-fat foods: Fried foods, fast food, heavy cream sauces
  • Excessive sugar: Candy, soda, pastries
  • Alcohol: No alcoholic beverages at all

Behaviors to Avoid

  • Intense exercise: No gym, running, or sports
  • Staying up late: Prioritize sleep
  • Skipping meals: Your body needs fuel
  • Excessive caffeine: More than 1-2 cups
  • Smoking/vaping: Limit as much as possible

Common Mistakes the Day Before

  1. Eating heavy late-night meal: Fatty foods take time to clear your system
  2. Having "just one drink": Any alcohol affects hydration and plasma
  3. Staying up late: Sleep deprivation shows in vital signs
  4. Forgetting to hydrate: Dehydration is a major deferral cause
  5. Intense gym session: Depletes nutrients you need
  6. Skipping dinner: Low protein the next day
  7. Not planning meals: Leads to poor food choices

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I eat the day before donating plasma?

Eat iron-rich foods like red meat, beans, or leafy greens, plus protein at every meal. Have a balanced dinner with protein, complex carbs, and vegetables. Avoid extremely fatty or fried foods.

How much water should I drink the day before plasma donation?

Drink 8-10 glasses of water throughout the day before donation. Proper hydration helps with blood flow and can affect your hematocrit reading. Avoid alcohol which causes dehydration.

Should I exercise the day before donating plasma?

Light exercise is fine, but avoid intense workouts the day before. Heavy exercise can affect your vital signs and iron levels. Save your hard workouts for after you've recovered from donation.

Can I drink alcohol the night before plasma donation?

Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours before donation. Alcohol dehydrates you, affects your vital signs, and can impact the quality of your plasma. Even moderate drinking can cause deferral.

What should I avoid eating before plasma donation?

Avoid high-fat and fried foods which can cause lipemic (fatty) plasma. Also avoid excessive sugar, alcohol, and caffeine. These can affect your screening results or plasma quality.

How much sleep do I need before plasma donation?

Aim for 7-8 hours of sleep the night before. Poor sleep affects your vital signs (especially pulse and blood pressure) and can lead to deferral. Rest is important for a successful donation.
