Can You Donate Plasma If You're Anemic?

Last Updated: January 2026

Understanding iron levels, hematocrit, and donation eligibility

üìÖ Updated: January 2026 ‚Ä¢ ‚è±Ô∏è 6 min read

üéØ Quick Answer

If you have clinically diagnosed anemia, you typically cannot donate plasma until your levels return to normal. However, if you have borderline-low iron or have been deferred before, there are steps you can take.

Each visit includes a hematocrit (or hemoglobin) test. You must meet minimum levels to donate that day.

What Plasma Centers Test For

Every time you donate, the center checks your hematocrit (percentage of red blood cells in your blood) or hemoglobin (the protein that carries oxygen). These indicate your iron status and your body's ability to safely donate.

Test Minimum for Women Minimum for Men
Hematocrit 38% 39%
Hemoglobin 12.5 g/dL 13.0 g/dL

Note: These minimums may vary slightly by center. Some centers test hemoglobin, others test hematocrit. Both measure essentially the same thing‚Äîyour body's oxygen-carrying capacity.

Types of Anemia and Donation Eligibility

üçΩÔ∏è Iron-Deficiency Anemia (Most Common)

Caused by insufficient iron intake, heavy menstruation, or chronic blood loss. This is often temporary and treatable.

Eligibility: May donate once iron levels normalize (usually 1-3 months with treatment)

üß¨ Sickle Cell Anemia

A genetic condition affecting red blood cell shape. This is a permanent deferral condition.

Eligibility: Permanent deferral - cannot donate plasma

üî¨ Other Chronic Anemias

Includes aplastic anemia, hemolytic anemia, and anemias caused by chronic disease. These require medical evaluation.

Eligibility: Varies - typically requires clearance from your doctor and the center's medical director

‚ö° Borderline-Low Iron (Not Anemic)

Your levels are at or just below the donation threshold, but you're not clinically anemic. Very common, especially in women.

Eligibility: Often can improve levels with diet/supplements and donate soon

If You've Been Deferred for Low Levels

Getting deferred for low hematocrit/hemoglobin is common and doesn't mean you can never donate. Here's what to do:

  1. 1

    Don't panic

    Low levels on one test don't mean you have anemia. Hydration, time of day, and recent diet all affect results.

  2. 2

    Ask how long to wait

    Most centers allow you to try again after 24-48 hours for a borderline result, or 7 days for lower results.

  3. 3

    Boost your levels naturally

    Eat iron-rich foods, stay well-hydrated, and avoid iron-blocking substances (coffee, tea) before your next attempt.

  4. 4

    If it happens repeatedly, see a doctor

    Multiple deferrals may indicate underlying anemia or other conditions that need medical attention.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will donating plasma make my anemia worse? ‚ñº
Plasma donation doesn't remove red blood cells (they're returned to you), so it doesn't directly worsen anemia. However, the screening exists to ensure you're healthy enough to donate. If you're borderline, frequent donation could stress your body. The screening test protects you.
Can I take iron supplements before donating to pass? ‚ñº
Iron supplements won't raise your levels in a day. It takes weeks of consistent supplementation to increase iron stores and improve hematocrit. If your doctor has recommended iron supplements, take them as directed‚Äîbut don't expect immediate results for donation.
I have sickle cell trait (not disease). Can I donate? ‚ñº
Sickle cell trait (carrying one copy of the gene) is different from sickle cell disease. People with the trait can usually donate plasma, though some centers may have specific policies. Disclose this during screening, and the staff will determine eligibility.
My levels fluctuate. Why am I accepted some days and not others? ‚ñº
Hematocrit varies naturally based on hydration, time of day, recent meals, menstrual cycle (for women), altitude, and even body position. If you're borderline, drink plenty of water the day before, eat iron-rich foods, and try donating at the same time of day for more consistent results.
