  • Evening (after 5pm): After classes end
  • Weekends: More flexible timing

Study While Donating

One of the best things about plasma donation: you can study during the 45-60 minute donation.

What You Can Do While Donating

  • Read textbooks (physical or e-reader)
  • Watch lecture videos (bring headphones)
  • Review flashcards (Anki, Quizlet on phone)
  • Listen to podcasts related to your major
  • Do online homework (on phone or tablet)
  • Write papers (if you can type one-handed)

What to Bring

  • Phone with headphones (charged!)
  • Portable charger
  • Textbook or e-reader
  • Snacks for after
  • Sweatshirt (donation rooms are cold)

Staying Healthy as a Student Donor

The College Donor Challenge

College life can make plasma donation harder:

  • Irregular sleep schedules
  • Dining hall diet (not always protein-rich)
  • Alcohol consumption
  • Stress during exams

Student Donor Tips

  1. Hydrate consistently: Keep a water bottle with you always
  2. Eat protein: Eggs, chicken, beans at dining hall
  3. Skip donation if hungover: You'll feel terrible
  4. Sleep before donating: At least 6 hours
  5. Avoid donating during all-nighters: Safety first

Student Tax Situation

Yes, plasma income is taxable, but as a student, your situation may be simpler:

Key Points

  • Standard deduction: If your total income is under $14,600 (2026), you likely owe no federal tax
  • Keep records: Track all donations and payments
  • 1099 forms: You'll get one if you earn $600+ at a single center
  • Mileage deduction: Track miles driven to the center
Tax Tip for Students

If plasma donation is your only income and you earn under $14,600/year, you likely won't owe federal taxes. But you should still file a return to build your tax history.
