What to Expect at Your First ImmunoTek Visit

Your first donation visit takes 2-3 hours. Here's exactly what happens, step by step:

1

Arrival & Registration (15-20 minutes)

When you arrive at your ImmunoTek center, you'll check in at the front desk. Bring the required documents:

  • Valid government photo ID (driver's license, state ID, or passport)
  • Proof of current address (utility bill, bank statement, or lease dated within 30 days)
  • Social Security card or SSN documentation

Staff will scan/copy your documents and create your donor profile in their system. You'll be assigned a donor ID number.

2

Health Questionnaire (20-25 minutes)

You'll complete a detailed health history questionnaire covering:

  • Current medications and supplements
  • Medical conditions and past surgeries
  • Travel history (certain countries may temporarily defer you)
  • Tattoos, piercings, or recent dental work
  • Risk factors for infectious diseases

Be honest on this questionnaire. It protects both you and plasma recipients. ImmunoTek staff will review your answers privately.

3

Physical Examination (15-20 minutes)

A medical professional will conduct a mini-physical to ensure you're healthy enough to donate:

  • Weight check: Minimum 110 lbs (determines your pay tier)
  • Vital signs: Blood pressure, pulse, and temperature
  • Protein test: Finger stick to check total protein levels (need 6.0+ g/dL)
  • Hemoglobin test: Ensures adequate iron levels (12.5+ women, 13.0+ men)
  • Vein check: Examines arms to ensure good veins for donation

If you fail any screening (low hemoglobin, high blood pressure), they'll explain next steps and when you can try again.

4

Orientation Video & Consent (10-15 minutes)

ImmunoTek requires all new donors to watch an educational video explaining:

  • How plasma donation works and what plasma is used for
  • Safety protocols and sterile equipment procedures
  • Potential side effects (rare but possible)
  • Donation frequency rules (twice per week, 48 hours apart)

After the video, you'll sign consent forms acknowledging the risks and agreeing to ImmunoTek's donor policies.

5

First Plasma Donation (45-60 minutes)

Now you're ready to donate. Here's what happens:

  • Donation chair: You'll recline in a comfortable donation chair (bring headphones for entertainment)
  • Needle insertion: Phlebotomist inserts needle into arm vein (slight pinch, then minimal discomfort)
  • Plasmapheresis cycle: Machine draws blood, separates plasma, returns red blood cells (repeats 3-6 times)
  • Donation time: First visit takes 45-60 minutes; future visits are faster (30-45 minutes)

Staff monitors you throughout. If you feel lightheaded, nauseous, or dizzy, tell them immediately. They can slow the machine or pause donation.

6

Post-Donation & Payment (10-15 minutes)

After donation completes:

  • Needle removal & bandage: Staff removes needle, applies pressure, and bandages your arm
  • Recovery area: Sit for 5-10 minutes, have a snack and drink to replenish fluids
  • Payment processing: Compensation loads onto prepaid debit card (issued on first visit)
  • Schedule next visit: Book your second donation (must be 48+ hours later)

Your first visit payment ($75-$100 for most new donors) should appear on your card within minutes. You can use the card immediately at ATMs or stores.

Pro Tips for Your First Visit

  • Drink 8-10 glasses of water the day before and morning of your visit
  • Eat a protein-rich meal 1-2 hours before (eggs, chicken, fish, nuts)
  • Avoid fatty foods for 24 hours before (can make plasma cloudy and cause deferral)
  • Get a good night's sleep (fatigue increases side effect risk)
  • Bring headphones, a book, or download Netflix shows for donation time
  • Wear comfortable clothing with easy access to both arms

Total Time: Plan for 2.5-3 hours for your first ImmunoTek visit. Subsequent donations take only 60-90 minutes total since you'll skip registration, orientation, and most of the physical exam.

ImmunoTek Payment Methods: How You Get Paid

ImmunoTek uses a prepaid debit card system for all donor payments. Here's everything you need to know about getting your money.

Prepaid Debit Card

  • ImmunoTek issues a reloadable prepaid card on your first visit
  • Payment loads automatically after each donation
  • Works like any Visa/Mastercard debit card
  • Use at ATMs, stores, online shopping, or bill payments
  • No social security number required for the card (already on file from registration)

Payment Timeline

  • Instant: Payment loads within 5-15 minutes after donation completes
  • Check balance: Use mobile app or call card service number
  • ATM access: Withdraw cash immediately at in-network ATMs
  • Purchases: Swipe for purchases same day as donation

ATM Withdrawal Tips

Maximize your cash access and minimize fees:

  • In-network ATMs: Find free ATMs using the card's mobile app (usually major banks like Bank of America, Chase, Wells Fargo)
  • Out-of-network fees: $2-$3 per withdrawal at non-partner ATMs, plus ATM owner's fee
  • Cash back at stores: Get cash back during purchases at grocery stores and gas stations for free (alternative to ATM fees)
  • Daily withdrawal limits: Most cards limit $500-$1,000 per day (call card service to request increases)
  • Monthly fee: Some cards charge $1-$2.50/month if balance remains on card (spend it down or transfer to bank account)

Transfer to Bank Account

Want to move money from your ImmunoTek card to your personal bank account?

  • Mobile app transfer: Link your bank account in the card's mobile app and transfer funds (may take 1-3 business days)
  • PayPal/Venmo/Cash App: Add your prepaid card as a payment source, send money to yourself (instant for a small fee, or free but takes 1-3 days)
  • Money order: Use card to buy money order at post office or grocery store, deposit into bank

Lost or Stolen Card: Call the card service number immediately (printed on back of card). They'll freeze the card and mail a replacement within 7-10 business days. Your balance is protected.

ImmunoTek Donation Requirements

Basic Eligibility

  • 18-64 years old (some states 18-69)
  • Minimum weight: 110 lbs
  • Valid government photo ID (driver's license, passport, state ID)
  • Proof of current address (utility bill, bank statement, lease within 30 days)
  • Social Security card or tax documents
  • Complete health questionnaire and physical exam

Health Screening Standards

  • Hemoglobin: 12.5+ g/dL (women), 13.0+ g/dL (men)
  • Blood pressure: 90/50 to 180/100 mmHg
  • Pulse rate: 50-100 beats per minute
  • Body temperature: Under 99.5F (37.5C)
  • Protein levels: 6.0+ g/dL total protein
  • Negative viral marker tests (HIV, Hepatitis B/C, etc.)

First Visit Duration: Your first donation at ImmunoTek takes 2-3 hours due to paperwork, physical exam, and orientation video. Subsequent visits typically take 60-90 minutes total (15-20 minutes screening, 45-60 minutes donation).
