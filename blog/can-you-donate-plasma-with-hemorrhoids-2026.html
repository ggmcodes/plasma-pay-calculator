Can You Donate Plasma With Hemorrhoids?

Usually yes, with some considerations in 2026:

  • Non-bleeding: Generally OK
  • Active bleeding: May need to wait
  • Comfort: Sitting for 45-90 min
  • Common condition: Many donors have them

Why Hemorrhoids Usually Don't Disqualify

Key points:

  • Not contagious: Cannot transmit
  • Plasma quality: Unaffected
  • Separate issue: From donation site
  • Very common: Millions have them

Frequently Asked Questions

I have hemorrhoids but they're not bleeding - can I donate?

Yes! Non-bleeding hemorrhoids don't affect your plasma donation eligibility at all. The main consideration is your comfort sitting in the donation chair for 45-90 minutes. Most people find the semi-reclined position comfortable.

My hemorrhoids bleed sometimes - does that disqualify me?

Not necessarily. Minor occasional bleeding usually doesn't prevent donation. However, regular blood loss can lower your hemoglobin levels over time. You'll need to pass the hemoglobin test at each visit. If you're concerned, consider iron supplements.

Will sitting during donation make hemorrhoids worse?

The donation chairs are semi-reclined, which actually puts less pressure on the area than regular sitting. Most donors with hemorrhoids find the position comfortable. You can bring a cushion if it helps.

I use Preparation H - can I still donate?

Yes, over-the-counter hemorrhoid treatments like Preparation H, witch hazel wipes, and similar products are acceptable for plasma donation. These topical treatments don't affect your blood or plasma.

I had hemorrhoid banding/surgery - when can I donate?

Wait until you're fully healed, typically 4-6 weeks after procedures. You should be pain-free and no longer experiencing any bleeding from the procedure before donating plasma.

Common Condition

Hemorrhoids don't typically prevent plasma donation! Non-bleeding hemorrhoids are not an issue. If yours bleed occasionally, just make sure you pass the hemoglobin test. The semi-reclined donation chair is usually comfortable. Use your treatments as usual and bring a cushion if needed!
