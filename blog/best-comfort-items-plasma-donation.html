Pro Tip: Cold Saline Returns

During plasma donation, your red blood cells are returned with saline solution at room temperature. This can make you feel cold, especially toward the end of your donation. A blanket is one of the most requested items by experienced donors!

How to Use Compression Sleeves for Donation

Put on your compression sleeve immediately after the phlebotomist removes the needle and applies the bandage. Wear it for 2-4 hours post-donation to minimize bruising. This is especially helpful if you tend to bruise easily or donate twice weekly.

Why Plasma Donation Causes Dry Lips

Plasma is about 90% water. When you donate, your body temporarily loses fluid, which can cause mild dehydration symptoms like dry lips. Staying well-hydrated before and after donation helps, but lip balm provides immediate relief during your visit.

Build Your Donation Comfort Kit

Regular donors recommend keeping a dedicated bag with all your comfort items ready to go. Include: travel pillow, blanket, compression sleeve, earbuds, lip balm, and a stress ball. Having everything ready makes it easy to grab and go on donation days!
