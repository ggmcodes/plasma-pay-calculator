What Disqualifies You from Plasma Donation? Complete List 2025

Comprehensive guide to plasma donation disqualifications including medical conditions, medications, travel restrictions, and lifestyle factors that may prevent you from donating plasma.

📅 Updated: January 2025 ⏱️ Read Time: 12 minutes ✅ FDA Guidelines

Quick Navigation

🏥 Medical Conditions 💊 Medications & Supplements 🦠 Infectious Diseases ✈️ Travel Restrictions 🚭 Lifestyle Factors ⏱️ Temporary Deferrals 🚫 Permanent Deferrals ➡️ What to Do If Deferred

Understanding what disqualifies you from plasma donation can save you time and help you prepare for your donation appointment. Plasma centers follow strict FDA guidelines to ensure donor safety and plasma quality. This comprehensive guide covers all major disqualification factors updated for 2025.

Important Notice

Disqualification criteria can vary between plasma centers and may change based on current health guidelines. Always consult with center staff for the most current requirements.

🏥 Medical Conditions That Disqualify Plasma Donation

Cardiovascular Conditions

•
Heart Disease: Any diagnosed heart condition including coronary artery disease, heart failure, or irregular heartbeat
•
High Blood Pressure: Uncontrolled hypertension (typically above 180/100 mmHg)
•
History of Heart Attack or Stroke: Any previous cardiovascular events
•
Blood Clotting Disorders: Hemophilia, von Willebrand disease, or other bleeding disorders

Autoimmune & Chronic Conditions

•
Diabetes: Type 1 diabetes or insulin-dependent Type 2 diabetes
•
Autoimmune Diseases: Lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, multiple sclerosis, Crohn's disease
•
Kidney Disease: Chronic kidney disease or dialysis patients
•
Liver Disease: Hepatitis, cirrhosis, or chronic liver conditions
•
Cancer History: Active cancer or cancer treatment within the past 5 years (exceptions for some skin cancers)

Medical Screening Process

Mental Health Conditions

•
Severe Depression: Current major depressive episodes or recent hospitalization
•
Bipolar Disorder: Uncontrolled or recent episodes
•
Substance Abuse: Active addiction or recent treatment programs
•
Eating Disorders: Active anorexia or bulimia

💊 Medications That Disqualify Plasma Donation

Permanent Medication Disqualifiers

Blood Thinners Warfarin, Coumadin, Plavix, Xarelto
Insulin Any form of insulin for diabetes treatment
Growth Hormone Human growth hormone from 1963-1985
Immunosuppressants Medications for organ transplant patients

Temporary Medication Deferrals

Antibiotics Must complete course and wait 24-72 hours
Aspirin Must wait 2 days after last dose
Steroids Oral steroids require 30-day waiting period
Antidepressants Some require monitoring or waiting periods

Herbal Supplements & OTC Medications

•
St. John's Wort: Natural antidepressant - requires deferral period
•
Ginkgo Biloba: Blood-thinning effects may cause temporary deferral
•
High-dose Vitamin E: Can affect blood clotting
•
Fish Oil Supplements: High doses may temporarily disqualify

🦠 Infectious Diseases & Blood-Borne Pathogens

Permanent Disqualifications

HIV/AIDS: Any positive HIV test result
Hepatitis B: Chronic hepatitis B infection
Hepatitis C: Current or past hepatitis C infection
HTLV: Human T-lymphotropic virus
Chagas Disease: Caused by Trypanosoma cruzi parasite

Temporary Infectious Disease Deferrals

COVID-19 Recent infection or exposure (14-28 day waiting period)
Flu or Cold Must be symptom-free for 24-48 hours
Fever Any fever within 24 hours
Food Poisoning 72-hour waiting period after recovery
Dental Work 72 hours for routine cleaning, longer for surgery

Global Travel Restrictions Map

Mad Cow Disease (BSE) Areas

Permanent deferral for extended stays in certain areas during BSE outbreaks:

🇬🇧
United Kingdom: 3+ months between 1980-1996
🇪🇺
Europe: 5+ years cumulative time in certain European countries
🏛️
Military Bases: Extended stays at specific military installations

Other Travel Deferrals

  • Zika Virus Areas: 28-day waiting period after travel
  • Babesiosis Risk Areas: Certain regions may require deferral
  • International Travel: Recent travel may require health screening

🚭 Lifestyle Factors

Risk Behaviors

  • IV Drug Use: Any history of intravenous drug use (permanent deferral)
  • Sharing Needles: Any needle sharing for drugs, steroids, or other substances
  • Unsafe Sexual Practices: High-risk sexual behavior may result in temporary deferral
  • Recent Tattoos/Piercings: 4-month waiting period in non-regulated facilities

Physical Requirements

  • Weight: Must weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kg)
  • Age: Typically 18-65 years old (varies by center)
  • Pulse: Must be between 50-100 BPM
  • Blood Pressure: Must be within acceptable range (90/50 to 180/100)
  • Protein Levels: Must meet minimum protein requirements
  • Hematocrit: Must be within normal range

⏰ Temporary vs Permanent Deferrals

🕒 Temporary Deferrals

You can donate again after the waiting period:

  • • Cold/flu symptoms: 24-48 hours
  • • Antibiotics: 24-72 hours after completion
  • • Dental work: 72 hours
  • • Vaccines: 2-4 weeks (varies by vaccine)
  • • Travel to malaria areas: 12 months
  • • Tattoos/piercings: 4 months
  • • Pregnancy: 6 weeks after delivery

🚫 Permanent Deferrals

These disqualify you indefinitely:

  • • HIV/AIDS diagnosis
  • • Hepatitis B or C infection
  • • IV drug use history
  • • Certain heart conditions
  • • Mad cow disease exposure
  • • Some autoimmune diseases
  • • Growth hormone (1963-1985)

📋 What to Do If You're Deferred

Steps to Take After Deferral

  1. Understand the Reason: Ask staff to clearly explain why you were deferred
  2. Get Documentation: Request written information about your deferral
  3. Note the Timeline: Understand when (if ever) you can return
  4. Address the Issue: Take steps to resolve temporary disqualifying factors
  5. Follow Up: Contact the center when your deferral period ends

Tips for Future Donations

  • Keep Records: Maintain documentation of medical treatments and travel
  • Prepare for Screening: Bring ID, list medications, and relevant medical information
  • Be Honest: Always provide truthful information during screening
  • Ask Questions: Don't hesitate to ask about specific requirements

💡 Pro Tip

If you're temporarily deferred, use the waiting period to improve your health. Stay hydrated, eat protein-rich foods, and maintain good sleep habits to ensure you're in the best condition for your next donation attempt.

🛡️ Donor Rights and Legal Protections

✅ Your Rights as a Donor

  • • Right to Information: Full disclosure of risks, procedures, and compensation
  • • Right to Refuse: Stop donation at any time without penalty
  • • Right to Privacy: Medical information protected under HIPAA
  • • Right to Compensation: Receive agreed payment for completed donations
  • • Right to Safety: Sterile equipment and trained medical staff
  • • Right to Comfort: Breaks, restroom access, and refreshments

⚖️ Legal Protections

Federal Protections

  • • FDA oversight and regulations
  • • HIPAA privacy protection
  • • Anti-discrimination laws
  • • Worker safety standards

State Protections

  • • State health department oversight
  • • Consumer protection laws
  • • Payment dispute resolution
  • • Medical malpractice coverage

🚨 Report Issues

If your rights are violated, contact:

  • • Center Management: First point of contact for immediate issues
  • • FDA: 1-800-835-4709 for safety violations
  • • State Health Dept: For licensing and operational issues
  • • Better Business Bureau: For payment disputes
