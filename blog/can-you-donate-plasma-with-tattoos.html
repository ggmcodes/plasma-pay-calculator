ü¶† Why Do Tattoo Rules Exist?

Tattoo and piercing deferral periods exist to prevent the transmission of bloodborne pathogens like Hepatitis B, Hepatitis C, and HIV. The incubation period means these infections might not show up in blood tests immediately after exposure.

üö´ Common Tattoo & Plasma Donation Myths

‚ùå Myth: "Small tattoos have shorter wait times"

‚úÖ Truth: Tattoo size doesn't matter. The infection risk is the same whether it's a tiny dot or a full sleeve.

‚ùå Myth: "Tattoos from years ago disqualify you forever"

‚úÖ Truth: Only recent tattoos (within waiting period) affect eligibility. Old tattoos don't matter.

‚ùå Myth: "You can lie about recent tattoos"

‚úÖ Truth: Plasma centers check for visible tattoos and ask directly. Lying puts recipients at risk and can get you permanently banned.

‚ùå Myth: "All states have the same rules"

‚úÖ Truth: Rules vary significantly by state. What's allowed in California might not be allowed in Oklahoma.
