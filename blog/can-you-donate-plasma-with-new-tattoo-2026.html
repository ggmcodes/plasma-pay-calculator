Can You Donate Plasma With a New Tattoo?

It depends on where you got the tattoo and your state's regulations in 2026:

  • Regulated states: Often no wait if from licensed shop
  • Non-regulated states: 3-4 month deferral typically
  • Unlicensed shops: Usually 3-4 month wait regardless
  • Home/DIY tattoos: Longer deferral period

Why The Wait Exists

Health concerns with tattoos:

  • Blood-borne pathogens: Hepatitis B, C, HIV risk
  • Contaminated equipment: Unlicensed shops may not sterilize
  • Shared ink: Can transmit infections
  • Window period: Time for infections to show in tests

What About Piercings?

Similar rules apply:

  • Ear piercing (gun): Often no wait
  • Body piercing (needle): May require wait
  • Licensed shop: Reduces or eliminates wait
  • Self-piercing: Usually 3-4 month deferral

How to Minimize Wait Time

  1. Use licensed shops only: Check for state license
  2. Keep receipts: Proof of where you got tattoo
  3. Know your state: Research regulations beforehand
  4. Plan timing: Get tattoo after a donation cycle
  5. Ask the center: They'll tell you exact wait required

Frequently Asked Questions

I got a small tattoo - does size matter?

No, the size of the tattoo doesn't affect the deferral period. A small wrist tattoo has the same requirements as a full back piece. What matters is where you got it done and your state's regulations.

What if my tattoo is completely healed?

Healing status doesn't change the deferral period. Even if your tattoo is fully healed in 2 weeks, you may still need to wait 3-4 months. The wait is about the window period for infections, not healing.

Can I get a tattoo touch-up and still donate?

Touch-ups count as new tattoos for donation purposes. The same deferral rules apply. If you need touch-ups, consider timing them after you've taken a break from donating.

What about permanent makeup or microblading?

Yes, permanent makeup (eyebrows, lips, eyeliner) and microblading follow the same rules as tattoos. They involve needles and ink, so the same deferral considerations apply.

I didn't tell them about my new tattoo - is that okay?

Never lie on the screening questionnaire. Being dishonest can get you permanently banned from donation. It also puts plasma recipients at risk. Always disclose tattoos honestly.

Plan Your Ink

If you're a regular plasma donor, plan your tattoos strategically. Get inked at a licensed shop in a regulated state, and time it after you've completed a donation cycle. That way you'll minimize any deferral period!
