Can You Donate Plasma On Antibiotics?

Generally, you cannot donate plasma while actively taking antibiotics in 2026. The concern isn't usually the antibiotic itselfâ€”it's the underlying infection being treated. Key factors:

  • Active infection: Most infections disqualify you
  • Wait period: Usually 24-72 hours after last dose
  • Feeling well: Must be symptom-free
  • Infection type: Some require longer waits

Exceptions: When Antibiotics Are OK

Some antibiotic uses don't require waiting:

  • Topical creams: Skin antibiotics usually fine
  • Acne treatment: Long-term doxycycline often OK
  • Preventive use: If no active infection
  • Prophylaxis: Pre-surgical antibiotics after cleared

When Can You Resume Donating?

  1. Complete your antibiotic course - Don't stop early
  2. Wait 24-72 hours after last dose (varies by center)
  3. Be symptom-free - No fever, pain, or infection signs
  4. Feel well overall - Back to normal health
  5. Disclose at screening - Be honest about recent treatment

Why the Restriction Exists

Reasons plasma centers require waiting:

  • Infection risk: Active infections can affect plasma quality
  • Your safety: Donating while sick stresses your body
  • Recipient safety: Protect plasma product quality
  • Accurate screening: Antibiotics can affect tests
