How to Prepare (Don't Skip This)

Proper preparation dramatically increases your chances of passing the screening on your first attempt. Getting deferred means no pay today, so take these steps seriously.

✅ Day Before

  • ✓ Drink at least 64 oz of water
  • ✓ Eat iron-rich foods (red meat, spinach, beans)
  • ✓ Get 7-8 hours of sleep
  • ✓ Avoid alcohol completely

✅ Morning Of

  • ✓ Eat a solid breakfast with protein
  • ✓ Continue drinking water
  • ✓ Avoid fatty foods (affects plasma quality)
  • ✓ Wear comfortable clothes with easy arm access

❌ Common Reasons for Same-Day Deferral:

  • • Low protein levels (didn't eat enough)
  • • High blood pressure (caffeine, stress, dehydration)
  • • Low hematocrit/iron (eat iron-rich foods 2-3 days before)
  • • Temperature above 99.5°F
  • • Pulse outside 50-100 bpm range

How You Get Paid

All major plasma centers pay via prepaid debit card that you receive on your first visit. Funds are loaded immediately after each donation—no waiting for checks or direct deposit.

✅ You Can:

  • • Use at any store that accepts debit/Visa
  • • Withdraw cash at ATMs
  • • Transfer to your bank account
  • • Use immediately after donation
  • • Add to mobile wallet (Apple Pay, Google Pay)

⚠️ Watch For:

  • • Some ATMs charge $2-3 fees
  • • Transfer to bank may take 1-3 days
  • • Keep track of your card (replacement = $5-10)
  • • Each card issuer has different fee structures

💡 Fee-Free Cash Access Tips:

  • • Get cash back at grocery stores (usually no fee)
  • • Use in-network ATMs (check your card's app)
  • • Pay rent/bills directly with the card
  • • Transfer to bank and wait (avoids ATM fees)

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really get paid today? ▼

Yes. All major plasma centers load payment onto a prepaid debit card immediately after your donation. You can use the card at stores, ATMs, or for online purchases right away. No waiting for checks or bank transfers.

What if I don't have all the documents? ▼

Call the center first. Many accept alternative documents—a pay stub, voter registration, or vehicle registration can work as proof of address. For SSN, a W-2 form or official tax document may be accepted. Some centers are more flexible than others.

How often can I donate? ▼

You can donate up to twice per week, with at least 48 hours between donations. This means potentially 8 donations per month, maximizing your earning potential while staying safe.

Does plasma donation hurt? ▼

There's a brief pinch when the needle goes in, similar to a blood draw. Most people find it very tolerable. During the actual donation, you shouldn't feel pain—many donors read, watch TV, or use their phones.

What if I feel dizzy or sick after? ▼

Mild lightheadedness is normal, especially for first-time donors. The center will have you rest before leaving and provide juice/snacks. Staying well-hydrated before, during, and after donation significantly reduces side effects.

Can I go to different centers? ▼

No—you should only be registered at one center at a time. Centers share a national database to prevent "double-dipping." Attempting to donate at multiple centers is dangerous and can result in permanent bans.

Is plasma donation income taxable? ▼

Technically, yes—the IRS considers plasma payments as taxable income. However, centers don't issue 1099s unless you earn over $600 from other reportable sources with them. It's your responsibility to report this income.

What if I was deferred in the past? ▼

It depends on the reason. Temporary deferrals (low iron, recent tattoo) resolve over time. Permanent deferrals for certain health conditions or behaviors may apply to all centers. Call ahead to explain your situation—the center can tell you if you're eligible to try again.
