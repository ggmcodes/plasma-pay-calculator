Hydration Tips

Proper hydration is the #1 factor for fast donations:

24 Hours Before

  1. Start early: Begin hydrating day before
  2. 64+ ounces: Drink at least 8 glasses of water
  3. Avoid dehydration: Limit alcohol and caffeine
  4. Electrolytes: Sports drinks can help

Day Of

  1. 16+ oz morning: Big glass when you wake up
  2. Continue drinking: Water up until appointment
  3. Avoid excess caffeine: Can constrict blood vessels
  4. Clear urine: Sign you're well hydrated

Better blood flow = faster plasma extraction:

Stay Warm

  • Wear layers: Keep your body warm
  • Warm clothes: Long sleeves, jacket
  • Avoid cold AC: Ask for blanket if cold
  • Warm drink: Hot tea or coffee before (small amount)

Exercise Before

  • Light activity: Walk, light jog before appointment
  • Gets blood moving: Improves circulation
  • Not too intense: Don't exhaust yourself
  • Arm exercises: Pump your arms a few times

Good Veins

  • Hydration helps: Veins more visible
  • Warmth helps: Veins dilate when warm
  • Let them see: Show both arms if asked
  • Regular donating: Veins get "trained" over time

Smart Scheduling

When you go matters for wait times:

Best Times

  • Early morning: Right when center opens
  • Mid-week: Tuesday-Thursday less busy
  • Avoid weekends: Often most crowded
  • Avoid 1st/15th: Paydays bring crowds

Worst Times

  • Lunch hour: 11am-1pm often busy
  • After work: 5-7pm rush
  • Saturdays: Busiest day at most centers
  • Month end: People need money

Appointment Tips

  • Book online: Reservations beat walk-ins
  • Arrive early: 5-10 minutes before appointment
  • Complete health check: Do screening questions at home via app

During Donation Tips

Active Pumping

  • Squeeze the ball: Pump consistently
  • Steady rhythm: Don't squeeze too hard or too soft
  • Keep at it: Don't stop pumping
  • Both hands: Alternate if one arm tires

Stay Relaxed

  • Don't tense up: Tension slows flow
  • Deep breaths: Stay calm and relaxed
  • Entertainment: Watch shows, read, to stay relaxed
  • Comfortable position: Adjust if needed

Don't Move Arm

  • Keep still: Movement can slow machine
  • Arm flat: Don't bend at elbow
  • Ask for help: Staff can adjust if uncomfortable

What Slows Down Donation

  • Dehydration: #1 cause of slow donations
  • Being cold: Constricts blood vessels
  • High fat meal: Makes plasma lipemic (fatty)
  • Caffeine excess: Can constrict vessels
  • Anxiety: Tension affects blood flow
  • Not pumping: Machine needs consistent flow
