Can You Donate Plasma With Fatty Liver?

Fatty liver itself may not disqualify you in 2026, but:

  • NAFLD (mild): Often can donate
  • NASH: More severe, may not qualify
  • Lipemic plasma: High fat content can disqualify
  • Diet matters: What you eat affects plasma

The Lipemic Plasma Problem

This is the real issue for fatty liver patients:

What Is Lipemic Plasma?

  • Plasma that appears milky or cloudy
  • High fat/triglyceride content
  • Can't be used for medications
  • Results in donation being discarded

Why Fatty Liver Increases Lipemic Risk

  • Liver processes fats less efficiently
  • Higher baseline triglycerides
  • Diet affects it more significantly
  • May need stricter dietary prep

Dietary Preparation Tips

Extra important with fatty liver:

24-48 Hours Before Donation

  • Avoid: Fried foods, fast food, pizza
  • Avoid: Heavy cream, butter, cheese
  • Avoid: Bacon, sausage, fatty meats
  • Avoid: Ice cream, pastries
  • Choose: Lean proteins, vegetables, fruits
  • Choose: Whole grains, low-fat options

Day of Donation

  • Light breakfast: Oatmeal, fruit, eggs (no cheese)
  • No fatty foods: Skip the bacon
  • Stay hydrated: Water, not sugary drinks
  • Time matters: Donate before any fatty lunch

Signs Your Plasma May Be Lipemic

  • Previous rejection: Center told you plasma was cloudy
  • High triglycerides: Known from blood tests
  • Fatty meal before: Ate heavy foods
  • Staff comments: Mention about plasma appearance

Frequently Asked Questions

My doctor said I have mild fatty liver - can I donate?

Probably yes, if you can produce clear plasma. The condition itself isn't automatically disqualifying, but you may need to be more careful about diet before donation.

I've been rejected for lipemic plasma before - what now?

Try again with better dietary preparation. Avoid all fatty foods for 24-48 hours before your next attempt. Some people with fatty liver can donate successfully with proper prep.

Will donating plasma affect my liver condition?

Plasma donation doesn't significantly affect liver function. However, if you have advanced liver disease, consult your doctor before attempting donation.

Should I tell the center about my fatty liver?

Yes, be honest during screening. They'll evaluate whether you can donate. Many fatty liver patients donate regularly without issues.

Diet Is Key

The biggest factor for fatty liver patients is plasma quality. Strict dietary preparation before donation can make the difference between successful and rejected plasma.
