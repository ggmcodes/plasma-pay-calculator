Key Preparation Tips

Proper preparation can mean the difference between a smooth 45-minute donation and being deferred. Here's your complete guide to preparing for plasma donation in 2026.

The Day Before Donation

Hydration (Most Important!)

  • Drink 64+ ounces of water - Start hydrating 24 hours before
  • Avoid alcohol completely - Dehydrates you and can defer you
  • Limit caffeine - Coffee is okay but water is better
  • Electrolytes help - Sports drinks or electrolyte tablets

Nutrition

  • Eat protein-rich meals - Chicken, fish, eggs, beans
  • Include iron-rich foods - Red meat, spinach, fortified cereals
  • Complex carbs - Whole grains for sustained energy
  • Avoid fatty foods - Can make plasma lipemic (milky)

Rest

  • Get 7-8 hours of sleep - Being tired affects your vitals
  • Avoid strenuous exercise - Save the gym for after donation

Morning of Donation

2-3 Hours Before

  • Eat a solid meal - Never donate on an empty stomach
  • Continue hydrating - Drink another 16-32oz of water
  • Avoid fatty breakfast - Skip bacon, sausage, fried foods

What to Eat for Best Results

High-Protein Foods

Protein helps maintain your plasma protein levels:

  • Chicken breast, turkey
  • Fish (salmon, tuna)
  • Eggs (boiled or scrambled)
  • Greek yogurt
  • Beans and lentils
  • Lean beef

Iron-Rich Foods

Iron helps maintain hemoglobin levels to pass screening:

  • Red meat (in moderation)
  • Spinach and leafy greens
  • Fortified cereals
  • Beans and lentils
  • Tofu
  • Dark chocolate (bonus!)

Signs You're Not Hydrated Enough

  • Dark yellow urine (should be light yellow/clear)
  • Feeling thirsty
  • Dry mouth or lips
  • Slow vein flow during donation

What to Bring

Required Documents

  • Photo ID - Driver's license, state ID, or passport
  • Proof of address - Utility bill, bank statement, or mail (first visit)
  • Social Security card - Or document with SSN (first visit)

Recommended Items

  • Water bottle - Stay hydrated during the process
  • Phone/headphones - Entertainment during donation
  • Snack - For after donation
  • Warm layers - Centers can be cold
  • Book or tablet - First visit takes 2-3 hours

What to Avoid

24 Hours Before

  • Alcohol - Automatic deferral and dehydrates you
  • Fatty/greasy foods - Causes lipemic plasma
  • Excessive caffeine - Can affect blood pressure
  • Heavy exercise - Depletes fluids and electrolytes

Day of Donation

  • Smoking right before - Wait 30 minutes
  • Skipping meals - Low blood sugar = deferral risk
  • New medications - Inform staff of any changes

Tips for Faster Donation

  1. Squeeze the stress ball - Keeps blood flowing
  2. Stay warm - Warm arms = better vein access
  3. Stay relaxed - Anxiety can slow flow
  4. Pump your fist - When instructed by staff
