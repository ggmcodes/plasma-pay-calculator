‚ö†Ô∏è Common Scheduling Mistakes

Too frequent donations: Trying to donate every 48 hours exactly often leads to protein deferrals, fatigue, and longer recovery times. The 48-hour rule is a minimum, not a recommendation.

Inconsistent scheduling: Constantly changing donation days disrupts your body's adaptation and makes it harder to maintain consistent eligibility.

Ignoring bonus deadlines: Not planning around promotional deadlines can result in significant lost income, sometimes $100+ per month.

Poor timing around life events: Scheduling donations before important events, travel, or stressful periods can lead to complications or necessary cancellations.

üè• Health-First Scheduling

Listen to your body: Some donors feel fine with twice-weekly donations, while others perform better with more recovery time. Adjust your schedule based on how you feel.

Seasonal adjustments: During illness-prone seasons (winter) or high-stress periods (finals, work deadlines), consider reducing frequency temporarily.

Hydration planning: Schedule donations when you can maintain optimal hydration before and after. Dehydration affects both eligibility and recovery.

Sleep considerations: Adequate sleep before donation improves protein levels and reduces side effects. Plan donations around good sleep schedules.

Consistency bonuses: Some centers offer bonuses for consistent donation patterns. Maintain your schedule to qualify for these additional payments.
