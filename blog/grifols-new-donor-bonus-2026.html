How to Qualify for the Grifols New Donor Bonus

Eligibility Requirements

To qualify as a new donor at any Grifols center (Biomat, Talecris, or Interstate), you must meet these criteria:

  • Age: 18 years or older (19 in some states like Alabama and Nebraska)
  • Weight: Minimum 110 pounds (50 kg)
  • Health: Pass medical screening (vital signs, protein levels, hematocrit, infectious disease testing)
  • Previous donations: No donations at any Grifols-owned center in the past 6 months
  • Identification: Valid photo ID, proof of SSN, proof of current physical address (no PO boxes)

The "New Donor" Definition at Grifols

You qualify for the Grifols new donor bonus if any of the following are true:

  • You've never donated at Biomat, Talecris, or Interstate
  • You haven't donated at any Grifols-owned center in 6+ months
  • You've donated at a competitor (BioLife, CSL, Octapharma, KEDPLASMA) but not Grifols

You cannot actively donate at another plasma center while receiving Grifols' new donor bonus. All major plasma networks share donor databases — concurrent donations result in permanent deferral and forfeit of any unpaid bonuses.

Medical Screening Process

Your first Grifols visit covers comprehensive screening:

  1. Registration (15-30 minutes): Complete the new donor questionnaire, provide ID/SSN/address proof
  2. Medical history (20-30 minutes): Detailed health questionnaire with staff
  3. Physical exam (15-20 minutes): Blood pressure, pulse, temperature, vein assessment
  4. Blood testing (10-15 minutes): Protein, hematocrit, infectious disease screening

  6. First donation (45-90 minutes): Actual plasma collection

Plan for 2.5 to 3.5 hours on your first visit. Subsequent donations drop to 60-90 minutes once you're in the Grifols system.

Avoiding Deferrals That Cost You Money

A single deferral inside a 30-day window can wipe out $100-$140 from your bonus. The most common Grifols deferral reasons:

  • Low protein (most common): Eat 30-40g of protein 2-3 hours before donating; avoid fatty foods
  • Low hematocrit/hemoglobin: Iron-rich foods (red meat, spinach, beans), avoid caffeine before donation
  • High pulse/blood pressure: Arrive calm, avoid caffeine 2 hours before, get adequate sleep
  • Dehydration: 64+ oz of water the day before
  • Recent tattoos/piercings: Wait 4 months before donating
  • Medications: Review the Grifols medication list before any new prescription

For a complete deferral prevention guide, see our Ultimate First-Time Plasma Donor Guide.

Improving Donation Time

  • Hydrate aggressively: 80+ oz the day before increases plasma volume and flow
  • Stay warm: Wear layers; warmth improves blood flow rate
  • Squeeze the stress ball rhythmically: Maintains flow throughout the draw
  • Pick your dominant arm: Most people flow faster on that side
  • Skip caffeine before donating: Caffeine constricts vessels and slows flow
  • Elevate your feet post-donation: Maintains blood pressure, prevents lightheadedness
