Can You Donate Plasma With Lupus?

Generally no - lupus (SLE) typically disqualifies you from plasma donation in 2026:

  • Autoimmune condition: Affects plasma quality
  • Medications: Often on immunosuppressants
  • Flare risk: Donation may trigger flares
  • Antibodies: Abnormal antibodies in plasma

Why Lupus Disqualifies

Several concerns with lupus patients:

  • Abnormal antibodies: Lupus produces autoantibodies that would be in donated plasma
  • Plasma quality: Disease affects plasma composition
  • Recipient safety: Autoantibodies could affect patients receiving plasma products
  • Donor safety: Stress of donation may trigger flares
  • Medications: Most lupus drugs are immunosuppressants

Discoid Lupus Exception

Skin-only lupus may be different:

  • Limited to skin, doesn't affect blood
  • Often not on systemic medications
  • No internal organ involvement
  • Some centers may accept - check individually

Alternative Ways to Earn

If you can't donate plasma with lupus:

  • Lupus research studies: Clinical trials often compensate
  • Patient advocacy: Paid speaking opportunities
  • Lupus foundations: Ambassador programs
  • Remote work: Flexible for flare management
  • Disability benefits: Check eligibility if needed

Frequently Asked Questions

I have mild lupus controlled with Plaquenil only - can I donate?

This is a case-by-case situation. Plaquenil (hydroxychloroquine) is less immunosuppressive than other lupus drugs, but the lupus diagnosis itself may still disqualify. Contact centers directly.

My lupus has been in remission for years - can I donate?

Even in remission, the autoantibodies associated with lupus are typically still present. Most centers will still defer lupus patients regardless of disease activity.

I was diagnosed with lupus but my doctor says it might be something else - what do I do?

If your diagnosis is uncertain, work with your rheumatologist to clarify. During screening, report what you've been told. If lupus is ruled out, you may become eligible.

Important

Lupus is a serious autoimmune disease. Focus on managing your health first. There are other ways to earn extra income that won't stress your immune system.
