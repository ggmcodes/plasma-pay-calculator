Can You Donate Plasma With Arthritis?

Usually yes, but it depends on the type and treatment in 2026:

  • Osteoarthritis: Generally eligible
  • Rheumatoid arthritis: Depends on medications
  • The condition itself: Usually not disqualifying
  • Medications: Some may cause deferral

Why Biologics Disqualify

Understanding the restriction:

  • Immunosuppression: These drugs lower immune response
  • Plasma safety: May affect plasma quality
  • Recipient concerns: Unknown effects on recipients
  • FDA guidelines: Most centers follow strict protocols

Osteoarthritis - Usually OK

Good news for OA:

  • Not autoimmune: Wear and tear condition
  • OTC treatment: Usually just pain relievers
  • No biologics: Typically not on immunosuppressants
  • Very common: Millions donate with OA

Donation Tips for Arthritis

  1. Know your meds: Bring a list to the center
  2. OTC is OK: NSAIDs don't disqualify
  3. Flare-ups: Consider waiting if in acute pain
  4. Arm flexibility: Need to straighten arm for needle
  5. Comfortable position: You'll sit for 45+ minutes
  6. Ask center: Specific meds, specific answers

Physical Considerations

Can you comfortably donate?

  • Arm extension: Must straighten arm fully
  • Grip strength: Need to squeeze ball periodically
  • Sitting time: 45-90 minutes in chair
  • Temperature: Centers are often cold

When to Wait

Consider postponing if:

  • Active flare: Significant joint inflammation
  • Recent steroid injection: Wait a few days
  • Severe fatigue: RA can cause exhaustion
  • Feeling unwell: General illness

Frequently Asked Questions

I have osteoarthritis and take Advil daily - can I donate?

Yes, osteoarthritis with OTC NSAIDs like Advil (ibuprofen) or Aleve (naproxen) is not a barrier to plasma donation. These common pain relievers don't affect eligibility.

I have RA and take methotrexate - can I donate?

Unfortunately, methotrexate is typically a deferral medication for plasma donation. It's an immunosuppressant that affects how your body processes plasma. You would need to stop the medication (with doctor approval) and wait before donating.

I'm on Humira for psoriatic arthritis - why can't I donate?

Biologics like Humira are TNF inhibitors that significantly affect the immune system. These medications remain in your plasma and could potentially affect recipients. Most plasma centers defer anyone currently taking biologics.

My arthritis only affects my hands - can I still donate?

As long as you can extend your arm fully and grip the squeeze ball, hand arthritis shouldn't prevent donation. The needle goes in your inner elbow area. If you have concerns about comfort during the process, speak with center staff.

I had arthritis as a child but don't take medication now - can I donate?

Yes, a history of juvenile arthritis that's now resolved without medication typically doesn't disqualify you. The key factors are your current health status and any medications you currently take.

Type & Meds Matter

Arthritis itself usually doesn't prevent plasma donation - it's the medications that matter most. Osteoarthritis with OTC pain relievers is typically fine. Autoimmune arthritis on biologics or methotrexate usually isn't eligible. When in doubt, bring your medication list to the center!
