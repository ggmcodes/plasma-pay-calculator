Is Plasma Donation Income Taxable?

Yes. Despite being called "compensation" rather than payment, the IRS considers plasma donation income to be taxable. It's treated as:

  • ‚Ä¢ Self-employment income (most common classification)
  • ‚Ä¢ Other income (sometimes reported this way)
  • ‚Ä¢ NOT a tax-free gift or charitable donation

Note: Some donors debate whether plasma income should be taxable (arguing it's compensation for time, not a sale). However, the IRS's current position is that it's taxable income. Failing to report it could result in penalties.

Understanding the 1099-NEC Form

When You'll Receive a 1099

Plasma centers must send you a 1099-NEC if you earned $600 or more from them in a calendar year.

You'll Get a 1099 If:

  • ‚Ä¢ You earned $600+ at one center
  • ‚Ä¢ Form arrives by January 31
  • ‚Ä¢ Also sent to the IRS

No 1099 If:

  • ‚Ä¢ You earned under $600 at each center
  • ‚Ä¢ But you still must report it!

Self-Employment Tax Considerations

If classified as self-employment income, you may owe self-employment tax (Social Security and Medicare):

Self-employment tax rate: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)

This is in addition to regular income tax on the earnings.

Setting Aside Money for Taxes

Consider setting aside 20-30% of plasma income for taxes. This covers both income tax and potential self-employment tax. Better to have extra than owe at tax time.

Year-End Tax Checklist

  • Total all plasma payments received during the year
  • Collect 1099-NEC forms from each center (arriving by Jan 31)
  • Compare your records to 1099 amounts
  • Total deductible expenses (mileage, parking, etc.)
  • Determine if you need to make estimated quarterly payments
  • Consult a tax professional if needed
