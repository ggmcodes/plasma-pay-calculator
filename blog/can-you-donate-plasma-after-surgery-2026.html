Can You Donate Plasma After Surgery?

You usually need to wait before donating plasma after surgery. The wait time depends on the type of surgery, how invasive it was, and your recovery. Key factors:

  • Surgery type: Minor vs major procedures
  • Full recovery: Must be fully healed
  • No complications: No ongoing issues
  • Off medications: Certain post-op meds may defer

Minor Procedures

Dental Work

  • Cleaning: Can donate same day
  • Fillings: Usually same day OK
  • Extractions: Wait 24-72 hours
  • Root canal: Wait until healed

Skin Procedures

  • Mole removal: After stitches out
  • Biopsy: After site heals
  • Cosmetic: Varies by procedure

Major Surgeries

Abdominal Surgeries

  • Appendectomy: 4-6 weeks
  • Gallbladder: 4-6 weeks
  • Hernia repair: 4-6 weeks
  • C-section: 6-8 weeks

Orthopedic Surgeries

  • ACL repair: 6-8 weeks
  • Joint replacement: 8-12 weeks
  • Fracture surgery: After healing

When You're Ready to Return

You can likely donate again when:

  1. Fully healed: Incision completely closed
  2. No infection: No signs of complications
  3. Off antibiotics: Course completed
  4. Normal activity: Back to regular routine
  5. Feeling well: No lingering symptoms

Permanent Deferrals

Some surgeries may permanently disqualify you:

  • Organ transplant: Recipient is deferred
  • Dura mater graft: Brain/spine tissue
  • Some blood transfusions: Depending on source
