Plasma Donation Frequency Limits by Country

How often you can legally donate plasma in the US, EU, Germany, Austria, Canada, and Australia, with annual maximums and the rules behind them.

Quick Answer

In the United States, the FDA allows plasma donation twice within any 7-day period (at least 48 hours apart), for up to about 104 donations per year. Canada matches this. Germany allows up to 60/year, Austria up to 50/year, the broader EU (EDQM) recommendation is about 33/year (25 liters), and Australia permits only about 24 to 26/year (roughly every 2 weeks). US and Canadian limits are among the most frequent in the world.

The 7-day window is a rolling period, not a calendar week. U.S. centers verify your history through the National Donor Deferral Registry (NDDR), so the twice-per-7-days and 48-hour rules are enforced even if you visit different centers.

Important Note

These figures reflect published national maximums and recommendations. Your own eligibility and schedule can be further limited by your weight, protein and hematocrit levels, health, and individual center policy. Frequency rules are set for donor safety. Always confirm current limits with your local plasma center.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often can you donate plasma in the United States?

The U.S. FDA allows plasma donation up to twice within any 7-day period, with at least 48 hours between donations. Donating exactly twice every week yields a maximum of about 104 donations per year, among the most frequent allowances in the world.

Why does the US allow more plasma donations than Europe?

Different regulatory philosophies. The EDQM recommends about 25 liters (roughly 33 donations) per donor per year across the EU, while the U.S. FDA permits twice per 7 days (up to 104/year). Some EU countries such as Germany set their own higher national limits.

How many times can you donate plasma per year in Germany?

Germany permits up to 60 plasma donations per year, with a required interval of two to three days between sessions, higher than the general EDQM recommendation of about 33 per year.

How often can you donate plasma in Australia?

Australia permits plasma donation roughly every 2 weeks, about 24 to 26 donations per year, far fewer than in the US or Germany.
