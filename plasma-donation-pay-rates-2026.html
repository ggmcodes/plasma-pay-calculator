Frequently Asked Questions

What is the highest paying plasma center in January 2026?

CSL Plasma currently offers the highest per-visit rates at $50-$120 depending on weight and location. For new donors, CSL's bonus program can pay up to $1,100 in the first month. BioLife is competitive at $40-$100 per visit with $900 new donor bonuses.

Did plasma pay rates increase in 2026?

Yes, several centers increased base rates for 2026. CSL Plasma raised second-visit payments by $5-$10 in most markets. BioLife increased new donor bonuses to $900 at select locations. Grifols implemented a new tiered system paying up to $105 for second weekly visits.

How much can I make donating plasma in January 2026?

Regular donors can earn $400-$800 per month in January 2026 by donating twice weekly. New donors can earn $900-$1,100 in their first month through bonus programs. Heavier donors (175+ lbs) earn 30-50% more due to larger plasma volumes collected.

Do I have to report plasma income on my 2026 taxes?

Yes, all plasma donation income is taxable as self-employment income. In 2026, centers issuing electronic payments (debit cards) over $600 will send you a 1099-K. You may deduct mileage (70 cents/mile in 2026) and other donation-related expenses.
