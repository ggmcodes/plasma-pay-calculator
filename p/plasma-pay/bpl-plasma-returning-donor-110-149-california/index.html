Frequently Asked Questions

How much does BPL Plasma pay per donation in California?

BPL Plasma pays approximately $115 per visit for donors weighing 110-149 lbs in California. This varies based on promotional periods and donation frequency.

How much can I make monthly donating plasma at BPL Plasma?

Donating twice weekly at BPL Plasma in California, donors weighing 110-149 lbs can earn approximately $996 gross or $785 after estimated taxes and travel costs.

What are the weight requirements for plasma donation at BPL Plasma?

You must weigh at least 110 lbs to donate plasma. Higher weight donors (110-149 lbs) often receive higher compensation because they can donate more plasma volume per session.

How often can I donate plasma at BPL Plasma?

You can donate plasma up to twice per week with at least 48 hours between donations. This means approximately 9 donations per month for maximum earnings.

Is plasma donation income taxable in California?

Yes, plasma donation income is considered taxable self-employment income. We've estimated 20.0% for taxes in our calculations. Centers issue 1099 forms for earnings over $600/year.

Do returning donors get bonuses at BPL Plasma?

Returning donors at BPL Plasma can earn bonuses through referral programs ($75 per referral), loyalty promotions, and special weekly/monthly incentives.

How long does a plasma donation take at BPL Plasma?

First-time donations take 2-3 hours including screening and physical. Subsequent donations typically take 45-90 minutes. The donation itself is about 35-45 minutes depending on your plasma volume.

What do I need to bring to BPL Plasma for my first donation?

Bring a valid photo ID, proof of address (utility bill or bank statement from last 30 days), and your Social Security card. Make sure you're well-hydrated and have eaten protein before your visit.
