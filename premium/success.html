The Bonus Stacking Strategy

You can legally donate at multiple centers (just not at the same time — centers check the national donor database). The key is finishing one center's bonus program before starting the next.

  1. Start with the highest-paying center near you (usually CSL or BioLife)
  2. Complete all 8 bonus donations within the bonus window (typically 30-45 days)
  3. Switch to center #2 and repeat. You're a "new donor" at each new center
  4. Continue through 3-5 centers over 4-6 months
  5. After exhausting bonuses, settle at whichever center has the best regular rates in your area
Important: Always be honest during screening. All major centers use the National Donor Deferral Registry (NDDR). Never donate at two centers in the same week — you must follow FDA rules (max 2x/week, 48 hours apart) regardless of which center you visit.

Part 3 — Plasma Donor Pro Toolkit

Pre & Post Donation Nutrition Plan

What you eat and drink directly affects donation speed, how you feel, and whether you pass the protein/hematocrit screening. Follow this plan to never get deferred for low levels.

24 Hours Before Donating

  • Hydrate aggressively: Drink 80-100 oz of water throughout the day (not all at once)
  • High-protein dinner: Aim for 30-40g of protein (chicken breast, eggs, beans, Greek yogurt)
  • Avoid: Alcohol, fatty/fried food, excessive caffeine, and salty snacks
  • Iron-rich foods: Spinach, red meat, lentils, fortified cereal, or an iron supplement

Morning of Donation

  • Breakfast (eat 2-3 hours before): Eggs + whole wheat toast + banana, OR oatmeal with protein powder + berries
  • Drink 16-20 oz water in the 2 hours before your appointment
  • Avoid: Coffee on an empty stomach (raises heart rate), energy drinks, sugary cereal

Post-Donation Recovery

  • Immediately: Drink 16 oz water or electrolyte drink. Eat the snack the center provides
  • Within 1 hour: Eat a protein-rich meal (at least 20g protein)
  • Rest of the day: Continue hydrating. Avoid heavy exercise for 4-6 hours
  • Iron recovery: Take an iron supplement with vitamin C (orange juice) to boost absorption

Eligibility Self-Assessment Checklist

Run through this before your first visit to avoid wasting a trip. If you check all boxes, you're almost certainly eligible.

Basic Requirements

  • I am between 18 and 69 years old
  • I weigh at least 110 pounds (50 kg)
  • I am in generally good health
  • I have a valid government-issued photo ID
  • I have proof of current address (utility bill, bank statement, or mail with name + address)
  • I have a Social Security number or valid ITIN

Health Screening (You Should Pass If...)

  • My temperature is below 99.5°F
  • My blood pressure is below 180/100
  • My pulse is between 50-100 bpm
  • I have not gotten a tattoo or piercing in the last 3 months (varies by state)
  • I have not had a blood transfusion in the past 12 months
  • I am not currently pregnant or have given birth in the last 6 months
  • I have not traveled to a malaria-risk country in the past 12 months

Part 5 — Plasma Tax Guide 2026

The Complete Plasma Donation Tax Guide

Plasma income is taxable. The IRS considers it self-employment income. Here's everything you need to know to stay compliant and keep more of what you earn.

How Plasma Income Is Taxed

  • Classified as: Self-employment / other income (not wages)
  • 1099-NEC / 1099-MISC: Centers issue these if you earn $600+ in a calendar year from one center
  • No 1099? You must still report the income. The IRS doesn't care whether you got a form
  • Tax rate: Your regular income tax bracket + 15.3% self-employment tax (Social Security + Medicare)

Reporting Step-by-Step

  1. Collect your 1099s from each center (usually mailed by January 31)
  2. Total up all plasma income including centers under $600 (use your tracker from Section 1)
  3. File Schedule C (Profit or Loss from Business) or Schedule C-EZ with your 1040
  4. File Schedule SE (Self-Employment Tax) if net earnings exceed $400
  5. Deduct eligible expenses (see deduction checklist below) to reduce taxable income

State-Specific Notes

Most states tax plasma income the same as the federal government. Notable exceptions:

  • No state income tax: TX, FL, NV, WA, WY, SD, AK, TN, NH — you only owe federal taxes
  • Low state tax: AZ, ND, PA — flat rates under 4%
  • Higher state tax: CA, NY, NJ, OR — factor in an additional 5-10%
